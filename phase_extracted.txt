Purpose Of This Doc: 
This doc converts the detailed info for each individual Phase/Stage  of the MM System’s Software/Platform from the “PHASE 1 AND PHASE 2 DETAILS” doc and covers expected outcomes, considerations, success metrics, and a detailed player-facing example of a player going through each phase/stage of the MM System platform. 


PHASE 1, STAGE 1
OVERVIEW/GOALS (both first time and returning players)
OVERVIEW / GOALS
Phase 1, Stage 1 is the entry point of the MM System, delivering the first of three transformation pillars: Awareness. Its purpose is to create the bridge between a player's current self and their future, upgraded self — by surfacing exactly what's holding them back and equipping them with a personalized, science-backed Golden Habit to address it.
It introduces players to gamified, AI-powered, science-backed habit transformation by:
Guiding players through the structured, multi-step Habits Hierarchy System (HHS) — an AI-powered conversational process that identifies key pain points across the 5 Cores and produces a fully personalized Golden Habit for each
Validating every Golden Habit against the Triple-Check Criteria: the player WANTS to do it, CAN do it, and it will be EFFECTIVE at addressing their specific challenge
Classifying each Golden Habit as either a Routine Habit (externally triggered, time/place-based) or a Non-Routine Habit (internally triggered, emotion/situation-based) — determining how it's organized, tracked, and which formation strategies apply
Building a Golden Habit Output that includes a precise cue/trigger, a built-in IF-THEN Obstacle Plan backed by one of the 3 MBMs, and an ultra-simple Starting Version for immediate implementation
Creating a dynamic, evolving knowledge base using the Command Center / Momentum Lists, unlocked as a reward at stage completion
This stage emphasizes simplicity, gradual progression, and deep self-awareness — ensuring players are set up for long-term success without feeling overwhelmed. Players start with one pain point and one Golden Habit, building the early momentum needed before expanding across all 5 Cores.
____
CORE SYSTEM COMPONENTS
AI-Powered Habits Hierarchy System (HHS): A structured, conversational discovery process that guides players through a deliberate multi-step progression — from pain point identification through Universal Principle alignment, habit personalization, and final Golden Habit construction. The AI adapts its conversation depth in real time: fast responders get an Express Mode flow; detailed responders get deeper exploration. The system continuously learns from individual player interactions and cross-player patterns to reduce friction and improve recommendations for everyone over time.
Routine vs. Non-Routine Habit Classification: Introduced during the HHS, this foundational classification determines how each Golden Habit is triggered, organized, tracked, and which MBM strategies apply. Getting the classification right — especially distinguishing emotional triggers from environmental ones — is one of the most critical factors in whether a habit sticks.
Triple-Check Validation: Every Golden Habit is validated against three criteria before it's locked in: the player genuinely WANTS to do it (intrinsic motivation), CAN do it given their real lifestyle (friction reduction), and it will be EFFECTIVE at addressing the confirmed pain point (behavioral science + cross-user data).
IF-THEN Contingency Planning: Built directly into the Golden Habit output. Before the player ever attempts the habit, their most likely obstacle is identified and a concrete MBM-backed fallback strategy is pre-loaded — dramatically increasing follow-through when friction inevitably shows up.
Progressive Momentum Lists Data Collection: As the player answers questions throughout the HHS, the AI automatically tags and routes responses to the appropriate Momentum Lists in the background — no manual organization required. By the time Stage 1 is complete, the player's Command Center is populated with structured, personalized self-knowledge ready to power ongoing AI recommendations.
Cross-Core Habit Relationship Mapping: Pain points and habits are anchored to one Primary Core but mapped to their ripple effects across the other four — ensuring the system understands holistic impact from day one.
____
KEY STAGE 1 OUTPUTS (what the player walks away with - Deliverables)
By the end of Phase 1, Stage 1, every player walks away with:
✅ One confirmed Primary Core and pain point, grounded in emotional resonance and behavioral root cause
✅ One personalized Golden Habit — classified (Routine or Non-Routine), with a precise cue/trigger, a Starting Version, and a built-in IF-THEN Obstacle Plan
✅ A Universal Principle and Core Dimension anchoring their habit in proven wisdom
✅ A populated Command Center with applicable Momentum Lists unlocked as a tangible reward
✅ Emotional buy-in and early momentum from a gamified progression experience that delivers immediate value

FIRST-TIME PLAYERS
The first-time experience is the longest version of Phase 1, Stage 1 because it serves three purposes simultaneously: (1) Onboarding — welcoming the player into the MM System; (2) Tutorial — demonstrating the methodology through the experience itself rather than explaining it separately; and (3) Golden Habit Formation — completing the full HHS to produce their first personalized Golden Habit.
Key elements of the first-time experience:
Epic Narrative & Mission Framing: Players are welcomed into an immersive sci-fi mission from the first moment — setting the tone that this is a game worth playing, not a questionnaire to endure. Key concepts (5 Core Areas, holistic balance, the Equation of Life, Momentum Lists) are introduced gradually as they become relevant, preventing cognitive overload
Full HHS Tutorial Mode: The player is guided through the complete structured hierarchy in tutorial mode. Each step builds naturally on the last — by the time the Golden Habit is locked in, the player has been walked through the entire methodology without ever feeling lectured at
Gamified Milestone Progression: Checkpoint animations, achievement titles, and Momentum Points are delivered at every stage of the HHS — training the player's brain from the start to associate self-discovery with positive reinforcement and dopamine hits tied to real growth
Command Center Unlock as Major Reward: The Command Center and populated Momentum Lists aren't handed to the player — they're earned through completing the HHS. This transforms a knowledge base into a trophy and gives players immediate, concrete proof of the self-knowledge they've built. The reveal is a full celebration moment with animation
What the player should feel by the end: welcomed, clear on their biggest pain point, confident that their Golden Habit is grounded in real science — and genuinely excited to continue rather than relieved it's over.

RETURNING PLAYERS
Returning players bypass all tutorial and narrative onboarding and go directly into the HHS for their next pain point. The experience is meaningfully different — not just shorter, but smarter. 
There are three distinct returning player pathways, each handled as a separate flow:
1. Voluntary New Habit Flow: 
Player has chosen to return on their own to tackle a new pain point. Motivated and self-directed.
AI opens with a brief recap of current Golden Habits and Momentum List status, then moves directly into pain point identification — Core Area introductions, Universal Principle explanations, and Routine/Non-Routine education referenced rather than re-taught
Existing Momentum List data reused aggressively — personalization questions targeted only at what's genuinely new for this habit
Cross-Core connections between the new pain point and existing habits surfaced proactively — showing the system's growing intelligence
Command Center update presented as a brief progression summary rather than a full unlock reveal
2. Guided Gamified Flow: 
Player knows their pain point and Core but wants the full structured HHS experience with narrative framing and gamified checkpoints — faster than first-time, but still immersive.
All checkpoint animations, achievement titles, and Momentum Point rewards preserved — onboarding and tutorial content removed entirely
Full HHS structure maintained with AI-guided discovery, but prior Momentum List data pre-loaded to reduce redundant questions
Golden Habit output quality identical to first-time flow, delivered in meaningfully less time
Command Center update presented as a progression summary rather than a full unlock reveal
3. Phase 2 Flag-Triggered Flow: 
Player is being referred back from Phase 2 because a habit has been flagged as struggling — 3+ consecutive days at or below a score of 3, or Captain's Log patterns triggering an AI intervention. Player may be frustrated, low-motivation, or skeptical. This flow must be fast, low-friction, and immediately reassuring.
All context from the flagged habit pre-loaded — player never has to re-explain what they were working on or why they're back
Three resolution paths presented immediately upon re-entry:
🚀 Quick Suggestion: AI suggests a fast tweak to the Golden Habit based on existing Momentum List data. No additional questions asked
🔬 Go Deeper: Structured HHS rebuild with all prior context pre-loaded. Player can rework the pain point framing, habit type classification, cue/trigger, or IF-THEN Obstacle Plan — or all of the above
✏️ Manual Edit: Player takes direct control and adjusts the Golden Habit parameters themselves
Framing throughout is empathetic and forward-focused — the system anticipated this moment and built it in; this is not a sign of failure
All refinement outcomes captured and routed back to applicable Momentum Lists and AI learning system
What the returning player should feel: a clear sense of progression — the system visibly knows them better, asks smarter questions, and makes stronger recommendations with every session. Not repeating a process. Leveling up.

🔧 TECHNICAL & DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS (Implementation Details)
NOTE: Full detail to be moved to Technical Specifications Document when created.
AI & Conversation Layer:
Chipp.ai currently powering AI interactions for testing and MVP; transitioning to VoiceFlow post-MVP with FlutterFlow handling frontend
Knowledge Docs architecture — one primary HHS Knowledge Doc anchoring all Stage 1 interactions, with supporting docs for 5 Core Areas, Universal Principles, Core Dimensions, Routine vs. Non-Routine classification, and Momentum Lists integration protocols
AI-First data collection: predefined high-value data points captured each session to identify friction (redundant questions, misleading prompts), refine Knowledge Docs, and improve cross-player pattern matching over time
Three distinct returning player conversation flows must be handled separately: voluntary new habit, guided gamified, and Phase 2 flag-triggered — each with appropriate context pre-loading from Firebase
Momentum Lists & Firebase:
Real-time auto-tagging (#ListType::CoreArea::TagLabel) routes all player responses to applicable Momentum Lists throughout the HHS conversation — not just at the end
Routine habits route to Routines Momentum List (organized by Morning/Afternoon/Evening time blocks and environmental clusters); Non-Routine habits route to Non-Routines Momentum List (organized by Core Area in priority order)
All Golden Habit data — type, cue/trigger, IF-THEN Obstacle Plan, Starting Version, MBM tags — stored at the habit level in Firebase and reflected across all views where habits appear
API-integrated across all phases — data entered in Stage 1 reflected system-wide immediately; architecture must support progressive Momentum List unlocking as player advances
Gamification & Animation Systems:
HHS checkpoint progression: Truth Seeker (+10 pts) → Core Confirmed (+15 pts) → Principle Decoder (+20 pts) → Keystone Forger (+25 pts) → Golden Habit Architect (+40 pts)
Each checkpoint triggers distinct celebration animation — escalating in intensity toward the Golden Habit unlock
Command Center reveal is a major celebration moment for first-time players: full animation sequence, Momentum Lists populating visibly, rocket visual with "breaking Earth's gravitational pull" framing
Routines List color system introduced at Stage 1 completion: 🔴 bad habit → 🟠 Golden Habit in formation → 🔵 MBMs attached → 🟢 fully formed
Feedback & Refinement Loop:
Phase 2 coaching engine monitors habit scores in real time; 3 consecutive days at or below 3 triggers AI flag and routes player back to Stage 1 via the flag-triggered flow
All refinement session outcomes captured and routed back to applicable Momentum Lists and AI Knowledge Docs for continuous system improvement
Reclassification support built at the habit level — if a habit is reclassified (Routine ↔ Non-Routine), Golden Habit carries over, MBMs are flagged for review, and scoring history is preserved with a dated audit note

⚙️ IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS (For Optimal Player Experience)
Player Experience Optimizations
Adaptive Pacing as a Core Design Principle: The AI must detect engagement signals in real time — response speed, answer depth, level of emotional detail — and adjust conversation depth accordingly. Fast, concise responders are routed into an Express Mode flow. Players who provide detailed, thoughtful answers receive deeper exploration and more nuanced follow-up. This is not optional — mismatching depth to the player is one of the primary causes of drop-off. The system carries the conversational weight; the player simply responds naturally.
Keep Interactions Appropriately Scoped:
First-time players: target under 10 minutes total for the full onboarding + tutorial + Golden Habit formation experience. The pain point "wow moment" should arrive within the first 5 minutes
Returning players (voluntary flows): target under 5 minutes
Phase 2 flag-triggered flow: target under 3 minutes from re-entry to resolution path selection
Demonstrate Immediate Value:
Show how information is being used in real time — the player should see tags being routed and lists being populated as they answer, not just at the end
Highlight personalization explicitly and frequently throughout the conversation
Reference the science and principles powering each suggestion — builds trust and differentiates from generic habit apps
Visual Recognition of Progress:
Clear progress indicators at every HHS checkpoint
Celebration animations tied to each section completion
"Unlocked" visuals for each Momentum List revealed at Command Center reward moment
Color-coded Routines List visual system introduced at Stage 1 completion: 🔴 bad habit → 🟠 Golden Habit in formation → 🔵 MBMs attached → 🟢 fully formed
Maintain Engagement Through Variety:
Mix question types: multiple choice, sliders, short text, confirmations
Include interactive elements at key decision moments (habit type classification, obstacle selection, Starting Version choice)
Incorporate gamified checkpoint moments throughout — never more than 2-3 questions between a reward or confirmation beat
Routine vs. Non-Routine Classification UX: This is the single most nuanced moment in the Stage 1 player experience. The system must clearly distinguish emotional triggers from environmental ones — a habit that appears routine on the surface may be non-routine if the real trigger is internal. The UX design for this step must make the distinction feel intuitive, not technical. Misclassification here creates downstream friction in MBM assignment, scoring, and Phase 2 tracking.
Technical Implementation Priorities
Knowledge Doc Architecture: AI responses throughout the HHS are powered by curated Knowledge Docs — not open internet pulls. One primary HHS Knowledge Doc anchors all Stage 1 interactions, with supporting docs for Core Areas, Universal Principles, Core Dimensions, Routine vs. Non-Routine classification, and Momentum Lists integration protocols. Maintaining these docs is as important as maintaining the code.
Data Storage & Auto-Tagging:
The tagging system (#ListType::CoreArea::TagLabel) is the mechanism driving all Momentum List population — every relevant player response is tagged and routed in the background throughout the conversation
Routine habits route to the Routines Momentum List (organized by Morning/Afternoon/Evening time blocks and environmental clusters); Non-Routine habits route to the Non-Routines Momentum List (organized by Core Area in priority order)
Design for expandability — more Momentum Lists unlock as the player progresses; the architecture must support this from day one
AI Response Generation:
Personalized, conversational tone throughout — the AI should feel like a knowledgeable guide, not a form
AI-First data collection: predefined high-value data points captured each session to identify friction (redundant questions, misleading prompts) and improve cross-player pattern matching over time
All three returning player pathways (voluntary new habit, guided gamified, Phase 2 flag-triggered) must be handled as distinct flows with appropriate context pre-loading
Visual Feedback System:
Supplement words with visuals and animation wherever possible to reduce text overwhelm
Checkpoint progression animations must be distinct and escalating — each one should feel more satisfying than the last
Command Center unlock must be a genuine celebration moment for first-time players — not a list appearing on screen

2️⃣ Habits Hierarchy Process: The 5 Steps to Your Golden Habit

PHASE 1, STAGE 2 (MBS - Momentum Boosting System)
OVERVIEW / GOALS
Phase 1, Stage 2 is the second pillar of the MM System's transformation framework: Friction Reduction. Its purpose is to take the Golden Habit established in Stage 1 and engineer it for real-world success — diagnosing the specific obstacles most likely to derail it, applying personalized behavioral science strategies to neutralize them, and setting up the player's environment so that executing the habit requires less effort than skipping it.
It advances the player from awareness to execution-readiness by:
Guiding players through the structured, 5-section Momentum Boosting System (MBS) — an AI-powered optimization process that applies the 3 Momentum Boosting Methods (MBMs) to each Golden Habit, targeting specific stages of the habit loop to make positive behavior the path of least resistance
Diagnosing the exact friction point most likely to cause the player to skip the habit — not the rational excuse, but the gut-level reaction — and building personalized MBM strategies to address it before the player ever encounters it in real life
Applying science-backed strategies drawn from James Clear, BJ Fogg, Charles Duhigg, and Peter Gollwitzer to manipulate the habit loop at the Cue, Craving, Response, and Reward stages — making the habit obvious, easy, and rewarding simultaneously
Executing or guiding all setup actions so the player leaves with a fully operational habit system, not a to-do list for later
Unlocking the Space Cantina as an earned milestone reward — connecting the player to a community of growth-focused individuals working on the same Cores, habits, and challenges
Continuing to build and enrich the Command Center through real-time Momentum List tagging throughout the MBS session
This stage emphasizes precision engineering over generic advice — every strategy is drawn from the player's own Momentum List data and applied to their actual daily reality. The output isn't a habit the player hopes will stick. It's a habit that's been systematically built to.

CORE SYSTEM COMPONENTS
Momentum Boosting System (MBS): The operational engine of Stage 2. A structured, 5-section optimization process that takes each Golden Habit from Stage 1 and transforms it into a Momentified Golden Habit — fully engineered with personalized behavioral science strategies, environmental design, and support systems. The MBS answers the question Stage 1 couldn't: not "what habit should I build?" but "how do I make it automatic?"
Obstacle & Opportunity Assessment: Before any MBM strategies are applied, the AI conducts a targeted diagnostic phase to surface the specific friction point most likely to derail the habit and the existing anchor behaviors available to leverage for success. The assessment distinguishes gut-level reactions from rationalized excuses — ensuring MBM strategies address the real blocker, not the one that sounds most reasonable.
3 Momentum Boosting Methods (MBMs): Using the obstacle-opportunity intelligence gathered above, the AI applies all three MBMs sequentially — each targeting a specific stage of the habit loop — to engineer the Golden Habit into the player's daily life.
The 3 MBMs, and the 3 strategies within each, include:
Make it Obvious/Attractive: Design the environment so the desired habit is the most prominent and appealing choice. Habit Loop Stages Addressed: Cue and Craving. 3 Strategies: Strategic Cue Placement, Context Engineering, Social Environment Design.
Make it Easy: Reduce response friction so the path of least resistance leads to the habit, not away from it. Habit Loop Stage Addressed: Response. 3 Strategies: Habit Stacking, Proactive Friction Reduction, MVA (Minimal Viable Action).
Make it Fun/Rewarding: Attach immediate emotional payoff to the habit so the brain marks it as worth repeating. Habit Loop Stage Addressed: Reward. 3 Strategies: Temptation Bundling, Pleasure Delaying, Celebrate Wins.
IF-THEN Contingency System: MVA (Minimal Viable Action) tiers are built into every MBM strategy — defining the smallest possible action that still preserves the habit loop on difficult days. Tiered backups (e.g., 5 reps → 3 reps → 10 arm circles) ensure the behavioral chain never fully breaks, even under suboptimal conditions, grounded in BJ Fogg's Tiny Habits research.
Personal Assistant Mode: Post-MVP AI agent capability that executes digital setup actions on the player's behalf — reminders, calendar events, playlists, small purchases, automations — removing all implementation friction at the moment of highest motivation. 
For MVP, the system presents the full setup action plan and guides the player through executing each action manually. 
Post-MVP, the AI Personal Assistant will execute digital actions autonomously (reminders, playlists, small purchases, calendar events) and provide step-by-step guidance for physical actions.
Continued Momentum Lists Integration: Stage 2 both draws from and contributes to the Command Center in real time. Momentum Lists populated in Stage 1 inform MBM strategy selection; new insights uncovered during the MBS (obstacles, anchor behaviors, tech preferences, routine details) are automatically tagged and routed to the appropriate lists — enriching the player's profile without any manual effort.
Space Cantina Social Hub: Unlocked as the culminating earned reward of Stage 2. A cosmic community hub organized around four pillars: Ideas Well (crowdsourced Golden Habit and MBM strategies with click-to-adopt), Interstellar Collaboration (Tribes, accountability partners, discussion forums), Leaderboards (anti-shame, multi-factor rankings), and Weekly Competitions (bonus points, credits, and real-life rewards). For MVP, links to the Moore Momentum Reddit community structured to mirror the Four Pillars.

⚙️ IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS (For Optimal Player Experience)
Player Experience Optimizations
Adaptive Pacing as a Core Design Principle: The AI must detect engagement signals in real time — response speed, answer depth, emotional detail — and adjust conversation depth accordingly throughout the MBS. Players who respond quickly and concisely are routed through a streamlined strategy selection flow. Players who provide detailed, thoughtful answers receive deeper exploration of obstacle root causes and more nuanced MBM options. This is not optional — mismatching depth to the player during the optimization process is one of the primary causes of drop-off between Stage 2 and Phase 2. The system carries the conversational weight; the player simply responds naturally.
Keep Interactions Appropriately Scoped:
First-time players: target under 15 minutes total for the full MBS including all 3 MBMs, setup actions, and Space Cantina introduction. The moment where the 3 MBMs combine into a unified habit system should arrive as a clear payoff within Section 3
Returning players (voluntary flows): target under 5 minutes — MBM tutorial content removed, AI leads directly into obstacle diagnosis and strategy selection
Phase 2 flag-triggered flow: target under 3 minutes from re-entry to resolution path selection — every second of friction here risks full dropout
The Obstacle Diagnosis Must Surface the Real Friction Point: The Section 2 assessment is the most consequential step in Stage 2 — if the wrong obstacle is identified, every MBM built on top of it will underperform. The AI must ask for the gut-level reaction that would cause the player to skip the habit, not the rational explanation they offer first. Where applicable, help the player recognize that skipping the habit doesn't actually save the time or energy they think it does. Ask up to one follow-up question if the initial response is vague — but never more. Misdiagnosis here creates downstream failure in Phase 2 that's difficult to trace back to the source.
MBM Selection Must Feel Personalized, Not Prescriptive: When the AI presents a strategy recommendation in Section 3, it must include specific, personalized implementation details — exact placement, exact timing, exact reward — drawn from the player's Momentum List data. A generic strategy name with no personalization ("try habit stacking") creates zero confidence. A specific, tailored suggestion ("after your 2 PM phone check, do 5 jumping jacks before you pick up your coffee") creates immediate buy-in. The science should feel like it's working for this player's actual life, not a textbook example.
Demonstrate Immediate Value Throughout:
Show Momentum List tags being routed in real time as new insights are discovered — not just at session end
Reference prior Momentum List data explicitly when it informs a recommendation: "Based on what you told me about your afternoon schedule..." builds trust and demonstrates intelligence
Highlight the science powering each MBM recommendation — builds credibility and differentiates from generic habit apps
For returning players, make the system's growing knowledge of them visible: reference patterns from prior sessions, cross-habit connections, and what's already been tried
Personal Assistant Mode Framing (First-Time Players): The tease in Section 1 and the full unlock in Section 4 must be handled carefully — the gap between tease and reveal needs to feel like earned anticipation, not forgotten promise. When Personal Assistant Mode unlocks, the capability boundaries (can do / cannot do / we'll do together) must be communicated clearly and confidently before offering to help. Setting accurate expectations here protects trust. For MVP, the manual guidance path must feel just as supported as the autonomous path will post-MVP — the player should never feel like they're getting a lesser experience.
Space Cantina Unlock Must Feel Earned: The Section 5 reveal for first-time players is the culmination of all of Phase 1 — not just Stage 2. The cosmic fanfare, doors opening animation, and Four Pillars overview should feel like a genuine milestone reward, not a feature walkthrough. Tribe matching should be personalized and presented with compatibility context (Core Area, habit type, active challenges) so the player's first community experience feels relevant rather than random. 1 | OVERVIEW / GOALS
Phase 2 is the execution engine of the MM System, delivering the third and final transformation pillar: Action, Accountability, and Automaticity. Where Phase 1, Stage 1 surfaced WHO the player is (Awareness) and Phase 1, Stage 2 engineered WHAT it takes to make their habits automatic (Vision), Phase 2 is where the player discovers HOW to actually get there — turning the personalized habits and Momentum Boosting Methods (MBMs) built in Phase 1 into consistent daily action through a fast, rewarding, ethically addictive daily ritual.
If Phase 1 chose the destination and fueled the rocket for success, Phase 2 is the mission itself: daily flight, navigation, course corrections, and the compounding rewards of sustained effort. It is where the player will spend the vast majority of their time in the MM System.
Phase 2 advances the player from execution-readiness to execution by:
Delivering a focused 5–10 minute daily check-in (Monday–Friday required, weekends optional) that scores all 5 Cores, captures Captain's Log reflections, delivers real-time AI coaching when patterns are detected, and rewards consistency with gamified progression
Serving as the player's all-in-one life command platform — a single unified Rocket Dashboard housing every key component: active Golden Habits, MBMs, Momentum Lists, the Trophy Room, the Space Cantina, full Phase 1 return access, and the entire Gamified Life Economy
Running a continuous AI coaching and pattern detection engine in the background — monitoring scores, Captain's Log entries, and habit performance to catch struggles before they become failures, then surfacing targeted interventions through three resolution paths (Quick Suggestion, Go Deeper, Manual Edit)
Maintaining holistic balance across all 5 Cores through the Balance Meter, Core Balance Alert system, and Balance Bonuses — making "firing on all cylinders" a daily, measurable, rewarded outcome
Building the Gamified Life Economy in full: Momentum Points, Space Credits, planet journey progression, ship upgrades (Wings, Armor, Thrusters), the streak system, Mystery Box rewards, the Trophy Room, badges, and achievements — all operating simultaneously and rewarding every meaningful action
Permanently linking back to Phase 1 — Stage 1 (HHS) and Stage 2 (MBS) remain accessible at any time, either proactively (player identifies a new pain point in another Core) or reactively (AI flags a struggling habit and routes the player back through Go Deeper)
This stage emphasizes daily simplicity and compounding rewards — a ritual so fast, engaging, and well-designed that coming back tomorrow never feels like a burden. Every design decision in Phase 2 answers one question: "Will the player want to come back tomorrow?"

2 | CORE SYSTEM COMPONENTS
Daily Review / Scoring Ritual (Core Experience): The central daily interaction of Phase 2 and the heartbeat of the entire MM System. A structured two-step morning check-in (5–10 minutes, Monday–Friday required, weekends optional) where the player scores each of the 5 Cores 1–5, logs wins and lessons in the Captain's Log, receives AI coaching when patterns are detected, then views a Progress Summary showing Momentum Points earned, streak status, planet journey progress, Balance Meter, and ship upgrade status. Designed around six simultaneous functions — single-pass scoring, backward reflection, forward priming, identity bridging (current self → Back to the Future vision), friction-minimized habit display, and core-level pattern detection — this ritual is where awareness becomes action, action becomes habit, and habit becomes identity.
Unified AI Intelligence (Mission Control): A single AI brain — the same one that powered Phase 1 — runs continuously in the background, monitoring scores, Captain's Log themes, and habit performance across sessions. Auto-flag triggers activate when: a habit scores at or below 3.0 for 3+ consecutive days, a Captain's Log negative theme recurs 3+ times, or a habit's success rate falls below 50%. When triggered, Mission Control offers three resolution paths: Quick Suggestion (fast tweak based on existing Momentum List data), Go Deeper (Phase 1 return for HHS rebuild or MBS refinement), or Manual Edit (player edits directly). All Phase 1 data informs Phase 2 coaching; all Phase 2 outcomes refine Phase 1 recommendations for future users — the bidirectional learning loop that powers the Data Learning Effects flywheel.
Navigation & Access Points (Rocket Dashboard): The Default Rocket Dashboard is the central hub — showing the player's rocket with earned upgrades visually applied, all five Core sections (unlocking progressively as habits are added), and the Daily Check-In button front and center. The Zoomed Out Progress Screen (showing current planet and distance to next) displays on log-in before zooming into the Rocket Dashboard. All primary navigation lives within two taps of the home screen: Hamburger Menu (Command Center, Space Cantina, Phase 1 return access, Settings), Trophy Room, four tip-of-rocket icons (Steering Wheel → Command Center, Clock → Routines List by time of day, Infinity → All Habits Quick View, Checklist → Non-Routines Detailed List), and the Stats Box (current planet, Momentum Score, Balance percentage).
Gamified Life Economy: The motivational engine powering Phase 2 — converting real behavioral progress into a layered reward system built around a space exploration narrative. Every mechanic is designed around a single guiding principle: dopamine hits must be tied to real progress, never fake achievements. Includes the Progressive Leveling System (Cadet → Navigator → Commander), Dual Currency System (Momentum Points to move the rocket; Space Credits to purchase ship upgrades), Planet Journey (Moon → Mars → Jupiter → Saturn → Pluto with checkpoint regression and milestone rewards), Ship Upgrades (Wings → Armor → Thrusters across Common/Rare/Very Rare/Epic tiers), Streak System (with Streak Savers, Armor grace periods, and Vacation Mode for protection), Variable Rewards & Mystery Box, Trophy Room & Habit Formation Milestones, Badges & Achievements, and Core Balance & "Firing on All Cylinders" mechanics.
Return Access to Phase 1: Phase 1 is permanently accessible from the main dashboard via the hamburger menu. Players can re-enter Stage 1 (HHS) to create new Golden Habits, or re-enter Stage 2 (MBS) to add or refine MBMs at any time — either proactively (voluntary return when the player's deepening awareness identifies a new pain point in another Core) or reactively (AI-flagged intervention via "Go Deeper"). All Phase 1 return sessions sync back to Phase 2 immediately upon completion. The AI pre-loads all relevant context — Momentum Lists, Captain's Log history, habit data — into any Phase 1 session initiated from Phase 2, ensuring the player never starts from scratch.
Routine vs. Non-Routine Habit Architecture (Continued): Both habit types — classified and established in Phase 1 — are tracked and scored daily in Phase 2. Routine Habits appear in the Daily Review/Scoring Ritual organized by time of day (Morning / Afternoon / Evening) within each Core; when formed, they turn 🟢 green in the Full Routines List and remain visible as part of the player's permanent automatic schedule. Non-Routine Habits appear below Routine Habits within each Core and graduate to the Trophy Room (organized by Core) when formed. The ALL HABITS QUICK VIEW (∞ icon on rocket dashboard) provides toggle flexibility between "By Time" and "By Core" layouts — accessible on demand without interrupting the check-in ritual. Every Golden Habit retains a Detailed View accessible throughout the experience, serving as the living master document for that habit.
Command Center & Momentum Lists (Continued): All Momentum Lists remain accessible and editable from the dashboard throughout Phase 2. Lists populated during Phase 1 continue to update through daily Captain's Log entries and Phase 1 return sessions. Progressive Momentum List unlocks continue at planet milestones via alien quests and Wings upgrades — Daily Bold Tracker, Messes to Clean, Tech Automations, Career Goals, and others. The AI cross-references all lists in real-time to generate increasingly precise coaching, intervention prompts, and habit sequencing recommendations. The longer a player uses Phase 2, the richer the data profile becomes — and the more powerful every AI interaction gets.
Trophy Room: The celebration of formed habits — accessible from the rocket dashboard. After 14+ days at 80%+ consistency, a habit becomes eligible for "formed" status, validated by AI cross-referencing scores and Captain's Log patterns. Formed Non-Routine habits move to the Trophy Room organized by Core; Routine Habits turn 🟢 green in the Full Routines List. Formation earns bonus Space Credits, a celebration animation, and the identity-framing message: "This habit is now part of who you ARE." A 4-layer passive monitoring system (monthly review prompts, Core average tracking, Captain's Log theme analysis, Balance Meter alerts) ensures formed habits stay genuinely formed over time — and triggers an empathetic un-form/rebuild path if they slip.
Space Cantina (Continued): Fully unlocked at Phase 1, Stage 2 completion and permanently accessible from the main dashboard. Players engage with all four pillars throughout their Phase 2 journey: Ideas Well (crowdsourced Golden Habit and MBM strategies with Click-to-Adopt), Interstellar Collaboration (Space Tribes, accountability partners, discussion forums), Leaderboards (anti-shame, multi-factor rankings), and Weekly Competitions (bonus Space Credits and real-life rewards). Community-validated habits and MBM strategies feed back into the AI's recommendation engine, amplifying the Data Learning Effects flywheel. MVP implementation uses Reddit as the Cantina platform; full in-app build comes post-MVP.

4 | FIRST-TIME PLAYERS
The first-time Phase 2 experience bridges the player from Phase 1 completion into daily execution. It is shorter than Phase 1's onboarding, but must immediately establish the daily check-in ritual as simple, rewarding, and worth returning for. The goal is for the player to walk away from their first session feeling that the mission has begun, the system is achievable, and they are genuinely excited to come back tomorrow — not because they have to, but because they want to see their rocket move and their habits build.
It serves two simultaneous purposes:
Tutorial — walking the player through the Rocket Dashboard and check-in ritual by doing it, not just describing it
First Check-In — completing the player's very first daily scoring ritual for the Golden Habit(s) established in Phase 1
Key Elements of the First-Time Experience:
"Mission Has Begun" Framing
The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 is positioned as the player's first real feeling of being in space, ready to tackle their next mission. The rocket is in orbit (they launched from Earth in Phase 1), the Golden Habit is fueled, and the mission itself is now beginning. Nova's opening message communicates that the player has completed preparation and is now ready for their epic journey to save themself by firing on all cylinders, then bringing back their knowledge to save Earth. The narrative escalation is earned — the player spent Phase 1 building the foundation; Phase 2 is where it activates.
Show, Don't Tell Tutorial
The player learns Phase 2 by being guided through it. The Rocket Dashboard is revealed through an interactive walkthrough, not a feature list. The Daily Check-In button is the first thing they tap. The ritual teaches itself through experience — within the first 8 minutes, the player has seen every primary component in use.
Gradual Unlock Reveal
Only Cores with active habits appear in color (will only be one after initial Phase 1 onboarding tied to the original pain point); others show ghosted lock icons with a visual invitation to "fuel this Core" by returning to Phase 1. This prevents overwhelm while naturally encouraging cross-Core expansion. Seeing greyed-out Cores creates a Pull — the player understands immediately what growing the system looks like, without feeling pressured to do it now.
Immediate Reward on First Check-In
The first scoring ritual — even with just one habit in one Core — earns Momentum Points and visibly moves the rocket toward the Moon. The payoff is instant, establishing the reward loop before the player has left the app. The Progress Summary after the first check-in must feel like a genuine win, not a placeholder.
Phase 2 Economy Introduction
Momentum Points, Space Credits, the planet journey, and the streak system are all introduced during the first session — not as a front-loaded lecture, but revealed progressively as each is earned for the first time. The player learns the economy by experiencing it, with Nova providing brief context at each new element's first appearance.
What the player should feel by the end: 
The mission has started, the system is simple and achievable, and they are genuinely excited to come back tomorrow — not because they have to, but because they want to see their rocket move, the new variable rewards they'll get, and their habits build.

5 | RETURNING PLAYERS
Returning players in Phase 2 are not returning to a one-time setup process — they are returning to a daily ritual that defines their entire ongoing experience in the MM System. Three distinct returning player states exist, each requiring meaningfully different handling from the system and producing a distinct player experience.

RETURNING PLAYERS — PATHWAY 1
Standard Daily Check-In Flow
The player is returning for their normal daily check-in. No gaps, no flags, no external triggers. This is the most common Phase 2 state — the everyday, routine experience that the entire system is optimized to make effortless and rewarding.
The System Should:
Load the Default Rocket Dashboard immediately — no tutorial, no re-explanation, no friction between the player and their ritual
Highlight the Daily Check-In button prominently if today's check-in hasn't been completed yet (front and center, impossible to miss)
Surface any pending AI insights, active habit flags (⚠️), or approaching streak milestones relevant to today's session in a brief, non-interrupting way
Deliver the two-step ritual smoothly: Step 1 (Score + Captain's Log + AI coaching if triggered) → Step 2 (Progress Summary + rewards + any Mystery Box reveal)
Keep the total session under 10 minutes for players with habits across all 5 Cores; under 5 minutes for early-stage players with 1–2 habits active
The Player Should:
Feel the ritual is fast, simple, and deeply familiar — opening the app and pressing Check-In should feel like muscle memory within the first week
Experience every session as accumulating identity evidence: "I showed up again. This is who I am."
Walk away with clear AI-generated focus reminders and a visible progress update (planet position, streak count, Balance Meter) that makes the day feel purposeful and grounded
Feel appropriately rewarded without the reward feeling hollow — every point earned is tied to real behavioral data, not just showing up

RETURNING PLAYERS — PATHWAY 2
Post-Gap Re-engagement Flow
The player is returning after missing two or more consecutive weekday check-ins. The rocket has regressed to its last checkpoint. The player may be low-motivation, guilt-ridden, or skeptical that returning is worth it. The experience must be fast, empathetic, and immediately forward-focused — relaunch, not punishment.
The System Should:
Acknowledge the absence without shame — Nova's opening message should be brief, warm, and forward-focused: "You're back. That's the only thing that matters right now. Let's relaunch."
Clearly show the relaunch state: current checkpoint position, and an explicit confirmation of everything that was preserved — Trophy Room, Space Credits, formed habits, badges, Momentum Lists — all fully intact
Frame checkpoint regression as "relaunching," never "losing" or "failing" — the system anticipated this possibility and built the protection in from day one
Offer a reduced-scope re-entry option for this first session back: just one Core, one habit, one Captain's Log entry — a minimum viable check-in that reactivates the streak without requiring full engagement immediately
Review any habits that should be flagged given the absence — AI scans scores and Captain's Log entries from before the gap and surfaces these proactively if patterns suggest a struggling habit, not just a schedule disruption
Capture re-engagement context: what caused the gap, what changed — routed to applicable Momentum Lists and AI pattern recognition to improve future intervention timing
The Player Should:
NOT feel punished, shamed, or like they've failed — the entire re-engagement experience must be empathetic and forward-focused from the first screen to the last
Feel immediate clarity: "Here's where I am. Here's what's preserved. Here's one small thing I can do right now."
Walk away with renewed momentum and a clear next session target — the gap treated as data, not defeat; the return treated as proof the system is worth coming back to
Experience this as evidence that the system is genuinely built for real human life — not a rigid program that collapses the moment life disrupts it

RETURNING PLAYERS — PATHWAY 3
Post-Phase-1-Refinement Flow
The player has just completed a Phase 1 return session — either a Stage 1 HHS rebuild (new or rebuilt Golden Habit) or a Stage 2 MBS refinement (updated or added MBMs) — and is re-entering Phase 2 with updated or new habits ready to track. This is a distinct use case requiring a specific bridge experience between Phase 1 output and Phase 2 activation.
The System Should:
Seamlessly surface all changes made during the Phase 1 session: new habits appear in the correct Core, updated MBMs are nested beneath them, Momentum Lists reflect all new insights — no manual re-entry, no syncing delay
Briefly acknowledge what changed and why — a Nova summary like: "Your [habit name] has been rebuilt with a new MBM strategy. Here's what's different and why it should work better." This validates the work the player just did in Phase 1
Position the return as a fresh experiment launch: the updated or new habit begins with a 3-day check-in cycle and is tagged as an active 🧪 Experiment until results are confirmed and it converts to permanent status
Deliver the standard daily check-in ritual incorporating the new or updated habit immediately — not "later" or "tomorrow," but right now in the same session
Update Mission Control's active context with all Phase 1 refinement data before generating Focus Reminders or coaching for this session — Phase 2 AI coaching must reflect the latest version of the player's habit system, not the previous one
The Player Should:
Feel a clear sense of progress — the refinement work they completed in Phase 1 is immediately visible, active, and integrated into Phase 2 tracking, not sitting somewhere in a settings menu
Experience the system as genuinely adaptive — it updated, acknowledged the change, and is already working with new information without the player lifting a finger to connect the two phases
Walk away with renewed confidence that the habit has been stress-tested and is more likely to succeed — and with a concrete 3-day experiment to validate that
NOT feel like they are restarting from zero — prior scores, Trophy Room contents, streaks, and rewards are fully preserved; only the struggling or new habit is changing

9 | ⚙️ IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS (For Optimal Player Experience)
Player Experience Optimizations
The Daily Ritual Must Feel Like a Game, Not a Chore
Every design decision in Phase 2 answers one question: "Will the player want to come back tomorrow?" The check-in must be fast enough to never feel like a burden, rewarding enough to always feel worth it, and personal enough that skipping creates genuine FOMO — not guilt. If a player ever consciously thinks "I have to do my check-in," the design has failed. The target feeling is: "I want to see my progress." This is the north star for every UX decision in Phase 2.
Friction Elimination as the Primary UX Goal
Check-in access must be ≤2 taps from app open — this is a non-negotiable design constraint
Push notification at player's set time should deep-link directly into Step 1 scoring — no intermediate screens
Habits display as abbreviated names during scoring — full details always available via [Detailed View] on demand, never forced into the ritual flow
Captain's Log is optional but prominently encouraged — never required, never blocking progress to the Progress Summary
All navigation from Progress Summary should be one-tap: Trophy Room, Space Cantina, All Habits View, Phase 1 return access
Shame-Free Accountability Architecture
Missed check-in day 1: encouragement message only, no consequences.
Missed check-in day 2: consequence (checkpoint regression) + relaunch framing, no further escalation.
Streak loss: always framed as "relaunching" — Trophy Room, Space Credits, and formed habit status are fully preserved regardless of streak state.
Score validation mismatches are surfaced privately as coaching prompts, never as public flags or corrections. The system can have teeth without having shame.
Balance Without Obsession
The system should encourage all 5 Cores without creating anxiety about imperfection. Core Balance Alerts appear only after 5+ consecutive days below 3.0 per Core — not as daily nudges. The Balance Meter is informational, not shaming. Players with only 1–2 active Cores should feel naturally invited to expand, never inadequate for not having all 5 active yet. The goal is pull, not push.
Identity-Building Through Ritual
Every Captain's Log entry is evidence accumulating toward the new identity. Nova's responses, the framing of formed habits as "this is who you ARE," the visible color progression from 🔴 to 🟢 across the Routines List — every UX moment should reinforce that the player is not just doing tasks; they are becoming someone new. This is the deepest form of retention.
Technical Implementation Priorities
Real-Time AI Integration
The AI must process Captain's Log data and score patterns within the same session — the Focus Reminders generated at the end of Step 1 must reflect what was logged in that exact session, not yesterday's data. Any meaningful latency in AI response at this moment will noticeably degrade the experience. This is one of the highest-priority technical requirements in the entire Phase 2 build.
Cross-Phase Data Sync
All changes made during Phase 1 return sessions must be reflected in Phase 2 immediately — not on the next session, not after a manual refresh. If a player rebuilds a habit in Stage 1 and immediately navigates back to their Rocket Dashboard, the rebuilt habit must already be there, tagged as an active experiment, and ready to score.
Progressive Momentum List Unlocking
New Momentum List unlocks at planet milestones must be delivered through the gamification system — not as silent background database updates. The player must experience the unlock as a celebration moment with alien encounter animation and Nova framing. Finding a new list in their Command Center without the unlock moment is a significant missed engagement opportunity.
Variable Reward Integrity
The Mystery Box system must be tied to genuine, complete check-in completion — both Step 1 and Step 2. Not just app opens, not partial interactions, not Step 1 alone. Anti-gaming measures are critical to maintaining the system's ethical addiction model: rewards must be tied to real behavioral data to retain their meaning. A reward earned for gaming the system is a reward that destroys the system's trust.
AI Pattern Detection Continuity Across Sessions
Mission Control's pattern detection must persist across sessions — a 3-consecutive-day low-score pattern detected on Day 5 should already have been building from Day 3. The AI should not "reset" between check-ins; it should be continuously evaluating against rolling windows so that interventions surface at exactly the right moment, not one day late.
