Document Overview
Purpose
This document is the gamification mechanics staging layer for the Moore Momentum PRD. It contains the detailed feature specifications — exact thresholds, rules, economy values, reward structures, and system behaviors — for every gamification feature in the MM System.
Its job is to hold these specs in a clean, organized, PRD-ready format so that when the relevant PRD sections are being written or updated, the content can be pulled directly without re-research or reconstruction.

What This Doc Covers
Exact mechanic specifications for every gamification feature: values, thresholds, rules, reward structures, economy balances, edge cases, and visual behaviors
Phase activation notes for each feature — how the feature behaves across Phase 1 Stage 1, Phase 1 Stage 2, and Phase 2
AI dependencies embedded in gamification features — only the AI logic inseparable from building that specific feature
PRD destination mapping — every section is tagged with the PRD section it feeds
Flagged placeholders (in bold red) identifying Phase 1 mechanic gaps where detail still needs to be confirmed before PRD authoring

What This Doc Does Not Cover
Full AI architecture and strategy. For the unified AI engine, Data Learning Effects, and competitive advantage framing, see the AI-First Strategy & Data Learning Guide.
Scientific or theoretical foundations. For why these mechanics work, see MM Science & Expert Foundations (Three-Layer Framework, Doc 1).
Design rationale and tradeoffs. For why specific design choices were made, see Product Design Rationale & Decision Log (Three-Layer Framework, Doc 3).
Player-facing walkthroughs or visual specifications. For step-by-step player experience and screen-level detail, see Phase 1 & 2 Sequence (Simplest for Coders).
Conceptual descriptions of what each mechanic is. For the what and why of each feature at a conceptual level, see Gamification, AI & Science: System Implementation (Three-Layer Framework, Doc 2).

How to Use This Doc With the PRD
Each section in this document is tagged with the PRD section it feeds. When writing or updating a PRD section, open the corresponding section here to pull exact specs. The PRD uses the A-G format (Current State, Issues, MVP Features, Post-MVP, Exploratory, Decisions, Technical) — this doc feeds primarily into C (MVP Features) and D (Post-MVP Features) of each relevant PRD section.

How to Read Feature Entries
Each section follows a consistent structure:
Feature Description — what it is and its primary purpose in the system

AI Dependency — AI logic inseparable from building this feature, with pointer to PRD Section 15 for the full AI spec

Note on PRD Migration
When a section of this document has been fully integrated into its corresponding PRD section, note the migration date next to the PRD destination tag and retain this document as a historical reference. Do not delete content after migration — this doc serves as the source record.

Section 1 — Space Narrative & Theme
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.1
Feature Description
The space narrative is the container for all gamification in the MM System. It is not a theme layered on top of the product — it is the product. Every mechanic, interface, and interaction is expressed through the lens of a cosmic mission. This framing elevates habit building from a chore into an epic personal journey with stakes, identity, and adventure.

MVP Specs
Core Narrative Premise
Planet Earth is in danger from the failure habits epidemic. The player must launch into the cosmos, discover universal principles, and return to save humanity.
Tagline: "Save Yourself, Save the Planet."
The player is the pilot. Their growth IS the mission.
Visual Metaphor Mapping
Rocket dashboard = the daily scoring interface — 5 Core sections are the ship's systems
Asteroid fields = obstacles in the player's path (low scores, missed check-ins)
Planets = milestones earned through sustained effort
Ship appearance = reflects real earned progress through upgrades purchased
Alien Guides = characters who deliver Universal Principles at planet milestones
Space Cantina = the social hub where players connect, compete, and collaborate
Narrative Integration Rules
Every reward, celebration, and alert uses space-language framing (e.g., "relaunching" not "failing," "checkpoint" not "setback")
Progress Summary after each check-in zooms out to show current position in the solar system and distance to next planet
All level names, achievement titles, and milestone language must be consistent with the space theme

Post-MVP Specs
Star Jump Engine unlocked after completing Pluto — opens future star system content beyond the solar system
Expanded alien character roster with distinct personalities and lore
Seasonal cosmic events tied to real-world calendar moments

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1:  Cinematic intro launches the narrative: rocket hovering in space, 5 Core icons visible then greyed/locked, Earth establishing shot, cosmic mission briefing. "Ready Player One?" and "Continue Game 🎮?" CTAs establish game language from the first moment. The Habits Hierarchy Pyramid morphs into the rocket at completion — rocket shown breaking Earth's gravitational pull. See Phase 1 & 2 Sequence doc for full visual walkthrough.
Phase 1, Stage 2:  Narrative continues as "Level 2." Opening visual shows rocket in orbit with the player's activated Core. Mission reframe: "Transform your habit from something you TRY to do into something AUTOMATIC." Space Cantina unlocks as the reward for completing Stage 2 — cosmic fanfare animation, doors opening to reveal the social hub.
Phase 2:  Full narrative activation. Rocket dashboard IS the daily interface. Planet progress shows after every check-in. Alien encounters trigger at planet arrivals. The mission is live and continuous — every check-in is a navigation session.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  Alien Guide messages at planet milestones are personalized — AI references the player's active Core focus and Momentum List data to contextualize the Universal Principle delivered. Generic messages are not permitted. See PRD Section 15 for full AI spec.

Section 2 — Progressive Leveling System: Cadet → Navigator → Commander
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.2
Post-MVP feature
Feature Description
The leveling system controls which features and capabilities players can access, scaling complexity with earned momentum. Players cannot access advanced features before building the habits and consistency required to use them well. Levels are permanent once earned — they can never be lost.

MVP Specs
Level Advancement Requirements
All three criteria must be met simultaneously — no single metric can be gamed:
Minimum number of habits moved to the Trophy Room (formed status)
Planet journey milestone reached (specific planet arrival)
Streak achievement threshold met (consecutive weekday check-ins at 4+ average)

Level Benefits
Cadet (Level 1 — Starting Level):
1x Space Credit earning rate
Access to Common-tier ship upgrades only
Basic feature set — core daily loop, Phase 1 HHS and MBS, Command Center
1 active Golden Habit per Core (expandable through progression)
Navigator (Level 2):
1.25x Space Credit earning rate on all credit earnings
Rare-tier ship upgrades unlocked
More habits per Core permitted
Optional daily challenges unlocked
Commander (Level 3):
1.5x Space Credit earning rate on all credit earnings
Epic-tier ship upgrades unlocked (Commander-only items)

Levels can never be lost or downgraded once earned — regardless of missed check-ins, low scores, or rocket regression

Level-specific challenges and achievements that only appear at each tier
Commander-exclusive cosmetic items and narrative content

Phase 1, Stage 2:  Still Cadet. Space Cantina preview at Stage 2 completion includes a brief introduction to the leveling concept (players can see what Navigator and Commander unlock).
Phase 2:  All level advancement occurs here. Level-up triggers are monitored continuously. Level-up celebration triggers when all three criteria are met simultaneously.

Section 3 — Dual Currency Economy: Momentum Points & Space Credits
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.3
Feature Description
Two separate currencies serve distinct psychological functions: Momentum Points are the progress engine tied purely to behavior (cannot be purchased or skipped), and Space Credits are the reward currency used to purchase upgrades and bonuses. Separating the two ensures that no player can buy their way through the journey — progression must be earned.

MVP Specs
Momentum Points — Progress Engine
Move the rocket forward through space toward the next planet
+10 points per completed weekday check-in regardless of scores — consistency matters most, not perfection
Cannot be purchased with Space Credits
Cannot be hoarded or banked to skip ahead — each check-in earns and applies in real time
Missing 2+ consecutive weekday check-ins triggers regression to previous checkpoint (see Section 6)
Progression pace by level:
Beginner (Cadet, first planet): Just 1 check-in reaches the first space station
Building Momentum (mid-journey): 3–5 check-ins to reach the next planet
Expert Mode (later planets): 7–10+ check-ins as challenges increase

Space Credits — Reward Currency
How Space Credits are earned:
Completing a daily check-in: base credit award
Hitting streak milestones (see Section 6 for milestone schedule)
Forming a habit — 🟠 → 🟢 transition (Trophy Room entry)
High score bonus: scoring 5/5 in any Core
Mystery Box rewards (see Section 7)
Quest completions (see Section 4)
Optional daily challenge completions (see Section 4)
What Credits Buy (Rocket Upgrades):

Wings (Unlocks Content):

Common Wings: Basic Momentum Lists at planets
Rare Wings: Advanced Momentum Lists + faster travel
Very Rare Wings: Elite content access
Epic Wings: All content + special abilities
Armor (Protects Progress):

Common Armor: 1-day grace period on streaks
Rare Armor: 2-day grace period
Very Rare Armor: 3-day grace + missed check-in protection
Epic Armor: Full week protection (emergency mode)
Thrusters (Accelerates Growth):

Common Thrusters: +25% Core Power gain per check-in
Rare Thrusters: +50% Core Power gain
Very Rare Thrusters: +75% Core Power gain
Epic Thrusters: +100% Core Power (2x speed)
Required Progression:
Must purchase in order: Wings → Armor → Thrusters
Must upgrade each tier before next tier unlocks
Can't buy Epic items until Commander level
Creates clear upgrade path and goals
Level Multipliers:
Cadet (Level 1): 1x credit earning
Navigator (Level 2): 1.25x credit earning
Commander (Level 3): 1.5x credit earning

Level multipliers applied to all Space Credit earnings:
Cadet: 1x
Navigator: 1.25x
Commander: 1.5x
Economy Balance Targets
Early players (Cadet) can afford meaningful Common-tier upgrades within approximately 2 weeks of consistent check-ins
Rare-tier items achievable within 1–2 months of consistent play
Epic-tier items require 3+ months of consistent commitment and Commander level

Credit gifting between tribe members (exploratory)

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1:  Momentum Points are introduced and earned at each Habits Hierarchy checkpoint: 10 pts (Truth Seeker) → 15 pts (Principle Decoder) → 20 pts (Keystone Forger) → 25 pts (Core Dimension Explorer) → 40 pts (Golden Habit Architect). Points are awarded with celebration animation at each checkpoint. Space Credits are not yet in play.
Phase 1, Stage 2:  Momentum Points continue: 10 pts (Friction Hunter) → 15 pts (Method Master) → 25 pts (Implementation Wizard). Space Cantina unlock at Stage 2 completion includes a 25 Space Credit welcome bonus. This is the player's first Space Credit experience.
Phase 2:  Both currencies fully active. Daily earning loop begins. Economy balance targets apply from Day 1.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  None directly for currency mechanics. Economy balance monitoring (detecting if players are accumulating too fast or too slow) is a product analytics function, not AI coaching. See PRD Section 20 for analytics spec.

Section 6 — Streaks & Accountability
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.6 — includes Ship Warnings / Progressive Accountability System
Feature Description
The streak system creates meaningful daily accountability through compounding rewards and real — but compassionate — consequences for missed check-ins. Multiple protection layers ensure accountability has teeth without triggering perfectionism or shame-based abandonment.

MVP Specs
Streak Definition
A streak requires consecutive WEEKDAYS (Monday–Friday) on which the player completes a check-in AND achieves a 4.0+ average score across all active Cores
Weekends (Saturday and Sunday) are always exempt — missing a weekend never affects the streak
Current streak count is displayed permanently at the top of the rocket dashboard
Streak Milestone Rewards
3 days: First momentum reward — variable reward trigger
7 days: Mystery Box bonus unlock
14 days: Ship upgrade unlocked (can now purchase Wings/Armor/Thrusters)
30 days: Major achievement + bonus Space Credits + Streak Saver earned
60 days: Elite status + special perks
90 days: "Momentum Master" achievement
180 days: Legendary status
365 days: Full celebration sequence — highest recognition in the system
[PLACEHOLDER — DETAIL NEEDED: Exact Space Credit bonus amounts and achievement badge details for each streak milestone — REVIEW AND CONFIRM]
[PLACEHOLDER — DETAIL NEEDED: Definition of "Elite status" perks at 60 days and "Legendary status" at 180 days — what specifically is unlocked — REVIEW AND CONFIRM]
Streak Protection Mechanisms
Protection layers stack — a player can use multiple protections in the same period:
Weekend Protection: Weekends are always exempt. Automatic — no player action required.
Streak Savers: Earned at the 30-day milestone. Automatically applied when the player misses 1 weekday, keeping the streak alive. Maximum of 1 Streak Saver banked at any time. Visual indicator shows "Streak Saver Available" when held.
Armor Upgrades: Common = 1-day grace period; Rare = 2-day grace period. Works separately from and stacks with Streak Savers.
Vacation Mode: Player activates before a planned absence. Pauses streak tracking for a defined period without penalty. Returns to pre-vacation streak state upon reactivation.
[PLACEHOLDER — DETAIL NEEDED: Vacation Mode details: how long can it be activated, how many times per year, whether there is a minimum streak length required to use it — REVIEW AND CONFIRM]
Ship Warnings — Progressive Accountability System
When weekday check-ins are missed, a graduated consequence system activates:
MISS 1 WEEKDAY CHECK-IN — Warning Only:
Warning notification appears. No consequences applied at this stage.
Streak remains intact. Momentum Points not lost. Ship position unchanged.
Player can defer the reminder by 1 hour (respects autonomy).
Warning message tone: supportive, never shaming. Example: "You missed your check-in yesterday. Your rocket is losing momentum. Check in today to stay on course!"
MISS 2 CONSECUTIVE WEEKDAY CHECK-INS — Consequence Triggered:
Rocket position regresses to the previous checkpoint (not the start of the journey)
Streak is broken if one existed
Momentum Points reduced
Tone remains supportive: "You've missed 2 check-ins. Your rocket has dropped back to the previous progress marker. Don't worry — your Trophy Room and all progress are safe!"
Always preserved regardless of consequence:
Space Credits balance
Trophy Room — formed habits are NEVER un-formed by a warning
All Momentum List data
Level and all earned achievements
[Get Support] options when the 2-miss consequence triggers:
Review Captain's Log for patterns
Simplify routine temporarily (Emergency Simplification Mode)
Connect with tribe in the Space Cantina
AI coaching session targeting obstacles
Temporary goal pause option if player is in a life crisis
[PLACEHOLDER — DETAIL NEEDED: Exact Momentum Point reduction amount triggered by 2 consecutive misses — REVIEW AND CONFIRM]

Post-MVP Specs
Additional Streak Saver earn opportunities beyond the 30-day milestone
Streak recovery challenges: complete a special challenge to partially restore a broken streak

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1 & 2:  No streak tracking. Streak concept is introduced conceptually — players see it as a Phase 2 feature during the Space Cantina preview at Stage 2 completion.
Phase 2:  Streak tracking begins on Day 1. All protection mechanisms are active from the start. Vacation Mode available immediately.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  The AI monitors for streak-risk patterns and proactively coaches before a streak breaks — e.g., flagging when a player's scores have been hovering near the 4.0 threshold for several days. The 2-miss consequence [Get Support] coaching session is AI-driven. See PRD Section 15.

Section 7 — Variable Rewards & Mystery Box

→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.7
Post-MVP feature
Feature Description
Variable rewards apply the psychological principle that anticipation generates more dopamine than the reward itself. The Mystery Box creates unpredictable delight within the daily check-in, making every session feel like it could surprise the player. Anti-gaming measures ensure rewards are tied exclusively to genuine behavioral engagement.

MVP Specs
Trigger Rules
10–15% chance of Mystery Box reward per completed check-in
Guaranteed Mystery Box reward every 10th consecutive check-in (prevents long dry spells that kill motivation)
Anti-gaming: reward probability requires a full check-in with Captain's Log entry completed — cannot be triggered by simply opening the app
Score validation cross-references Captain's Log before reward eligibility is confirmed — inflated scores do not unlock disproportionate rewards
Presentation Flow
Step 1: Mystery Box appears after check-in is complete (when triggered). "Tap to reveal your reward!"
Step 2: 2–3 second anticipation animation plays before the reveal. This delay is intentional — anticipation IS the experience.
Step 3: Reward revealed with celebration animation appropriate to the reward type.
Reward Mix: 70% Useful / 30% Delightful
Useful Rewards (70%):
Bonus Space Credits: +25 to +100
Streak Savers: protect one missed weekday check-in
Core Boosts: temporary +0.5 score multiplier for one Core for 24 hours
Upgrade Discounts: 10–20% off next ship upgrade purchase
Double Points: 2x Momentum Points for the next check-in only
Delightful Rewards (30%):
Mystery Cosmetics: ship skins, color variants, patterns
Personalized Alien Messages: Zorg or another Alien Guide references specific moments from the player's Captain's Log
Throwback Memories: "Remember Day 23 when you overcame X?" — AI-surfaced past Captain's Log highlight
Achievement Unlocks: special badges or titles not available through standard progression
Easter Eggs: hidden game references, humor, unexpected surprises
Personalized Inspirational Quotes: AI-generated based on the player's current challenges and progress
Personalized Alien Message — Design Note
This is the highest-value Delightful reward. The message references a specific moment from the player's Captain's Log history, creating emotional connection that no point system can replicate. Example:
"Captain, I've been watching your transformation. That moment on Day 23 when you chose the protein shake over cereal despite being rushed? That's when I knew you were becoming the person you're meant to be. The upgraded you is closer than you think. Keep firing on all cylinders! — Zorg, Planet Mars"

Post-MVP Specs
Expanded Delightful reward catalog as Captain's Log archive grows richer
Tribe-shared reward moments — option to share a Mystery Box reveal in the Space Cantina

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1:  No Mystery Box. Achievement checkpoint rewards (escalating Momentum Points with celebration animations) establish the reward psychology without variable mechanics.
Phase 1, Stage 2:  No Mystery Box. Space Cantina 25 Credit welcome bonus at Stage 2 completion is the bridge reward.
Phase 2:  Variable rewards fully active from Day 1. First possible Mystery Box trigger is on Day 1's check-in.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  Personalized Alien Messages and Throwback Memories require AI to: (1) scan the Captain's Log archive for emotionally significant moments, (2) draft the personalized message in the correct alien character voice, and (3) ensure the referenced moment is positive/growth-oriented. See PRD Section 15.

Section 8 — Trophy Room & Habit Formation Milestones
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17.8 — includes Habit Formation Goals / Dynamic Progression System

Feature Description
The Trophy Room is the player's permanent record of identity transformation — habits that have become automatic and are now part of who they are. It is the deepest form of progress visible in the system, more powerful than any score or point balance because it represents real behavioral change that has been sustained over time.

MVP Specs
Formation Eligibility Threshold
14+ days with 80%+ consistency (scoring 3 or above on the habit's Core on days the habit applies)
AI validation required before "formed" status is confirmed — behavioral data must match the claimed formation
Early formation option: player can manually mark a habit formed earlier with a confirmation prompt acknowledging the 2+ week research-backed standard
Trophy Room Display
Formed non-routine habits move to the Trophy Room, organized by Core (🧠 💰 👥 💪 🧘)
Routine habits that reach formed status turn green in the Full Routines List but stay visible in the daily schedule
Trophy counts are visible to tribe members in the Space Cantina — personal pride and social proof
Full Routines List color transformation — the most visceral progress indicator in the system:
🔴 Bad / Failure Habit — default, pre-system
🟠 Active Golden Habit — in progress
🟢 Formed Habit — lives in Trophy Room (or green in Routines List)
Formation Rewards
Celebration animation sequence
Bonus Space Credits awarded
Ship upgrade progress contribution
Trophy added to Trophy Room with identity language: "This is now automatic and part of who you ARE!"
[PLACEHOLDER — DETAIL NEEDED: Exact Space Credit bonus amount for habit formation — REVIEW AND CONFIRM]
Monthly Review System
Review prompts at 30, 60, and 90 days after formation ensure formed habits stay formed
Review checks: Core average monitoring (detects 1.0+ point drops), Captain's Log theme analysis, score trend review
Un-forming option is always available — framed as "adaptation, not failure," never a punishment
If formed habit slips, AI suggests un-forming and rebuilding with fresh insights from the player's evolved profile
Habit Formation Goals — Dynamic Progression System
Goal Setup:
System suggests a formation target: "Let's aim to form 1 habit in your first 2 weeks"
Player can adjust: 1 habit in 7 days (ambitious) through 1 in 21 days (cautious)
Research-backed defaults: 14–21 days for simple habits, 30–60 days for complex habits
Tracking:
Daily check-in updates formation progress bar showing days remaining and consistency percentage
"Closest habit to forming" always displayed with days and consistency data
At-risk alert: "Only 4 days left and no habits close to forming. Need help?"
Celebration When Goal Hit:
Confetti animation + specific habit name that was formed
Identity language: "This is now automatic and part of who you ARE!"
Rewards unlocked: credits, ship upgrade progress, achievement
Next challenge suggestion presented immediately
Dynamic Progression Scaling:
Beginner (Cadet): 1 habit / 14 days
Building Momentum: 2 habits / 30 days
Advanced (Navigator): 3 habits / 45 days
Expert (Commander): 4+ habits / 60 days

Post-MVP Specs
Trophy Room social features: browse tribe members' Trophy Rooms for strategy inspiration
Formed habit anniversary celebrations (e.g., 6-month and 1-year markers)

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1 & 2:  No Trophy Room activity. Golden Habits are being set up. Players see the Trophy Room referenced as a future destination during the Stage 2 Routines List visualization.
Phase 2:  Trophy Room tracking begins on Day 1. Formation eligibility monitoring starts immediately. First trophy is typically earned around Day 14–21 for most players.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  AI validates formation eligibility — cross-referencing scores, Captain's Log mentions, and behavioral patterns before confirming formed status. Monthly review monitoring is also AI-driven. Throwback memories in Mystery Box rewards reference Trophy Room milestones. See PRD Section 15.

Section 10 — Core Balance & "Firing on All Cylinders"
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 16

Feature Description
The Core Balance system ensures players cannot neglect any of the 5 Cores for extended periods. It enforces the MM System's foundational promise: holistic, balanced growth. A player who is excelling in one Core while neglecting others is not experiencing the system's full transformational power.

MVP Specs
Balance Meter
Displays 7-day rolling averages across all 5 Cores in the Progress Summary after every check-in
Visualized as fuel gauges — each Core has a bar showing its 7-day average on the 1–5 scale
Makes imbalance immediately obvious and actionable before it becomes a crisis
Display format:
🧠 Mindset: [bar] 4.2 / 5
💰 Career: [bar] 1.8 / 5 ⚠️
👥 Relationships: [bar] 4.6 / 5
💪 Physical: [bar] 2.1 / 5 ⚠️
🧘 Emotional: [bar] 3.5 / 5
Core Balance Alert — Trigger Rules
A red ⚠️ badge appears on any Core that scores below 3.0 for 5 or more consecutive days
Badge displayed on the rocket dashboard — impossible to miss
Alert persists until the Core scores above 3.0 for at least 2 consecutive days
Alert message includes:
Core name and number of consecutive days below threshold
Recent low scores displayed in context
[Review Habits] → Opens that Core's habit view for inspection
[Get AI Help] → Triggers targeted AI intervention for that Core
[Done] → Acknowledges the alert; player takes responsibility
Multi-Core Alert
If 2 or more Cores simultaneously fall below 3.0 for 5+ days, both show ⚠️ badges simultaneously
Combined alert adds: "Multiple Cores are struggling. This might indicate broader life stress. Consider simplifying or getting support."
[Emergency Simplification Mode] option appears in multi-Core alert
Emergency Simplification Mode
Temporarily pauses non-essential habits so the player can focus on a minimum viable routine across all Cores
Framed as a strategic reset, never as failure — language: "Let's simplify to keep your momentum alive"
Player selects which habits to pause; AI suggests which to prioritize keeping active

Balance Bonus Rewards
Bonus Space Credits awarded when all 5 Cores maintain above the threshold for a defined consecutive period

Post-MVP Specs
Deeper Ripple Effect analytics: AI shows which Cores rise and fall together for this specific player
Personalized balance recommendations based on player archetype patterns

Phase Activation
Phase 1, Stage 1 & 2:  Balance concept introduced at the system level — the 5 Cores framework and the importance of holistic balance are explained. No balance tracking active yet.
Phase 2:  Balance Meter and Core Balance Alert system both active from Day 1. First alert is possible from Day 5 onward if a Core is consistently neglected.

Section 12 — Sustaining Long-Term Engagement
→ Feeds PRD: PRD Section 17 — cross-cutting
Feature Description
This section captures the cross-cutting design principles and mechanics that prevent the experience from becoming stale over time. These are not individual features but emergent properties of the system working together — the result of all the above mechanics combining to keep engagement fresh, challenging, and identity-driven over months and years.

MVP Specs
Progressive Challenge Calibration
Each planet requires more momentum than the last — the challenge scales with the player
Each level (Cadet → Navigator → Commander) requires higher thresholds across all three advancement criteria
The system never becomes easy — there is always a next planet, a next level, a next trophy to earn
Expanding Content
New Momentum Lists unlock through planet arrivals and Wing upgrades — the Command Center grows richer over time
New alien encounters introduce new Universal Principles at each planet — wisdom content expands with progression
The system always has content the player hasn't seen yet
Visual Transformation Narrative
The color transformation (🔴 → 🟠 → 🟢) across the Full Routines List makes long-term progress viscerally visible
The rocket's evolving appearance through ship upgrades tells the story of the journey at a glance
The growing Trophy Room is a permanent, visible record of who the player has become
The Balance Meter's 7-day rolling averages show holistic improvement over time
Identity Anchoring — The Deepest Form of Retention
The player who once needed external motivation to check in eventually checks in because it is simply what they do
Behavior has merged with identity — the ultimate goal of the entire system
Trophy Room, formed habits, and the evolving BTTF vision all reinforce: "This is who I am now"
Long-term retention is not driven by new features — it is driven by the player's investment in their own transformation

Post-MVP Specs
Star system content beyond Pluto — new planets, new alien lore, extended Universal Principle library
Annual "Year in Review" celebration — AI-generated summary of the player's transformation over 12 months

Phase Activation
Phase 1:  Foundation for long-term engagement is set: identity-based framing, investment in Momentum Lists, cosmic mission narrative. The player is primed to want to come back.
Phase 2:  All long-term engagement mechanics active. The compound effect of daily wins across all 5 Cores is what sustains engagement — not novelty alone.

AI Dependency
AI Dependency:  The AI's continuous learning loop is the core engine of long-term personalization — recommendations improve over time as the player's data profile grows richer. The system that knows you better than you know yourself is the deepest retention mechanism. See PRD Section 15 for full AI spec.
