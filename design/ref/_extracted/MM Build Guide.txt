2.1 Brief Overview & Context
Purpose: Guide players through AI-powered questions to identify personalized Golden Habits via the 5-step Habits Hierarchy Journey. The goal is to move from vague pain point → specific, actionable Golden Habit in 3-5 minutes (for initial) and 1-2 minutes for returning, and to introduce the key personalization element of the Command Center/Momentum Lists. 
Visual Metaphor: Move through all 5 sections of the Habits Hierarchy pyramid until complete phase 1 where they see the pyramid was a hybrid for their rocket, which then launches at the end as they complete level 1. et.
Key Principle: Each step builds on the previous, no skipping allowed (creates investment and proper foundation).
The 5-Step Journey:
1. 	Pain Point Scanner - Identify what's holding player back
2. 	Core Area Mapper - Connect to one of 5 Core Areas
3. 	Principle Decoder - Identify underlying universal principle (build trust)
4. 	Keystone Personalizer - Match to personalized habit category (leap  from generic advice to highly personalized)
5. 	Golden Habit Forger - Create specific, actionable Golden Habit (solid actionable/simple starting point that feels achievable).

2.3 Features 1-10 (Detailed Specifications)
Feature 1: Multi-Agent AI Architecture (Voiceflow) ✅
Status: COMPLETE (in refinement)
What It Does:
Voiceflow-driven conversations with natural language processing. Multiple specialized AI agents hand off to each other based on conversation context. Adaptive questioning adjusts based on user responses. Context preservation allows users to leave and return without losing progress.
Technical Implementation:
•   	Voiceflow multi-agent setup with defined handoff points
•   	Session state management in Firebase
•   	NLP for understanding natural language responses
•   	Knowledge documents uploaded to each agent
UI Requirements:
•   	Chat interface (ListView for history, TextFormField for input)
•   	Progress indicator showing current step (1-5)
•   	Auto-scroll to latest message
•   	Typing indicator while AI responds
Success Criteria:
•   	Conversation feels natural (not robotic)
•   	Context preserved across sessions
•   	Response time < 3 seconds
•   	Handles unexpected inputs gracefully
Feature 2: Core Area Selection ✅
Status: COMPLETE
What It Does:
Player selects which of the 5 Core Areas to focus on first. Can be AI-recommended based on initial questions or player-chosen. Explains each Core Area briefly before selection.
The 5 Core Areas:
•   	🧠 Mindset: Growth-owner thinking, resilience, mental frameworks
•   	💰 Career & Finances: Professional growth, wealth building, purpose
•   	👥 Relationships: Deep connections, communication, support network
•   	💪 Physical Health: Energy, fitness, nutrition, sleep
•   	🧘 Emotional & Mental Health: Stress management, fulfillment, passion
UI Requirements:
•   	5 tappable Core Area cards with icons and brief descriptions
•   	Visual highlight on selection
•   	Option for AI to recommend based on initial questions
Feature 3: Pain Point Identification ✅
Status: COMPLETE
What It Does:
AI identifies specific pain points through open-ended questions. Starts with Core Areas scoring lowest. Uses "Why?" drilling technique to find root cause (not surface symptoms). Validates understanding before proceeding.
Example Flow:
AI: "What's holding you back from being your best self?"
User: "I'm tired all the time"
AI: "When you say tired, is it physical exhaustion, mental fatigue, or lack of motivation?"
User: "Mostly physical, I don't sleep well"
AI: "Got it - so poor sleep quality is affecting your energy. Let me confirm: your main challenge is getting quality sleep, which leaves you physically exhausted. Is that right?"
Technical Notes:
•   	NLP categorizes pain points to Core Areas (we have core area 
•   	Top Pain Points Database for pattern matching
•   	Clarifying questions if input is vague
Feature 4: Momentum List Collection ✅
Status: COMPLETE
What It Does:
Collects information for the 17 Momentum Lists during natural conversation flow. This data powers AI personalization throughout the system. Lists are stored in Command Center for player to view/edit.
The 17 Momentum Lists:

Feature 6: Golden Habit Recommendation ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
AI recommends personalized Golden Habits based on pain points, Core Area, and Momentum Lists data. Pulls from Master Habits Database (500+ vetted habits). Explains WHY this habit addresses their specific pain point.
Recommendation Algorithm:
•   	Match pain point → relevant habits in database
•   	Filter by player's strengths (leverage what they're good at)
•   	Filter by player's passions (use what they enjoy)
•   	Filter by time availability (fits their schedule)
•   	Filter by obstacles (avoid known friction points)
•   	Rank by predicted success rate
Dependencies:
•   	Master Habits Database (currently 60% complete)
•   	Auto-tagging system (Feature 5)
•   	Pain point → habit mapping rules
Feature 7: WANT/CAN/EFFECTIVE Scoring ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Triple-Check Criteria validation ensures every Golden Habit passes three tests before being recommended.
The Triple-Check:
•   	✅ WANT: Does player actually want to do this? Feels good, energizing, aligned with passions
•   	✅ CAN: Can player realistically do this? Fits schedule, resources, lifestyle constraints
•   	✅ EFFECTIVE: Will this actually address their pain point? Evidence-based, proven approach
UI Flow:
After AI recommends habit, player rates each criterion on 1-5 scale. If any score < 3, AI suggests modifications or alternatives. If all pass, proceed to habit creation.
Feature 8: Habit Refinement Options ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Allows player to customize, adjust, or regenerate Golden Habit recommendation until satisfied. Player should feel ownership over their habit, not like it was imposed.
Refinement Options:
•   	Tweak: Adjust frequency, duration, timing, or specific action
•   	Alternative: Show next-best habit from database
•   	Custom: Player enters their own habit (validated against criteria)
•   	Regenerate: Start recommendation process over
Feature 9: Master Habits Database Integration ❌
Status: NOT STARTED (Database 60% complete)
What It Does:
500+ vetted habits organized by Core Area, pain point, and difficulty level. Each habit includes why it works (behavioral science backing), common pitfalls, and success tips. AI queries database for personalized recommendations.
Database Structure:
•   	Habit ID, Name, Description
•   	Core Area(s) - primary and secondary
•   	Pain Points addressed
•   	Difficulty level (1-5)
•   	Time required (minutes)
•   	Science backing (citations)
•   	Success rate (from user data - Post-MVP)
Feature 10: Quality Feedback Collection ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
End-of-flow quality check: "How helpful was this Golden Habit?" (1-5 stars). Collects qualitative feedback: "What would have made this better?" Feeds Data Learning Effects to improve recommendations over time.
Data Collected:
Relevance score (1-5)
Personalization score (1-5)
Would implement: Yes/No/Maybe
Open text feedback
Time to complete Stage 1
Drop-off points (where did they exit?)
Why It Matters:
This data creates the competitive moat through Data Learning Effects. More users → better recommendations → higher success rates → more users. Critical for long-term differentiation.

SECTION 3: PHASE 1 STAGE 2 - 3 Momentum Building Methods
3.1 Overview & Context
Purpose: Apply the 3 MBMs (Make Obvious, Make Easy, Make Rewarding) to Golden Habits identified in Stage 1. This reduces friction and increases success rate before daily tracking begins.
Timeline: 10-15 minutes per Golden Habit
Status: NOT STARTED (all 10 features)
Key Outcome: User leaves Phase 1 with 2-3 Golden Habits per Core Area, fully optimized with 3MBMs, ready for Phase 2 daily tracking. Each habit has been "Momentified" - transformed from generic habit into personalized, friction-reduced, reward-enhanced action.
3.2 Issues This Phase Solves
Issue
Solution
Habits fail because they're too hard
Make It Easy - break into tiny steps, reduce barriers
Habits fail because people forget
Make It Obvious - environmental cues, reminders, triggers
Habits fail because they're not rewarding
Make It Rewarding - immediate rewards, dopamine hits
Obstacles derail progress
IF-THEN planning - proactive obstacle handling
3.3 Features 11-20 (Detailed Specifications)
Feature 11: MBM #1 - Make It Obvious/Attractive ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Explains the science of cues and triggers. Suggests personalized cue strategies based on player's Momentum Lists (schedule, environment, existing routines). Player selects and customizes their cue.
Types of Cues:
•   	Time-based: "Every day at 7am"
•   	Location-based: "When I enter the kitchen"
•   	Action-stacking: "After I pour my coffee"
•   	Emotional: "When I feel anxious"
•   	Environmental: "When I see my gym bag by the door"
Personalization Points:
Uses player's Time Availability list to suggest realistic timing. Uses Environment list to suggest physical cues. Uses existing routines for action-stacking opportunities.
Feature 12: MBM #2 - Make It Easy ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Explains the science of reducing friction. Suggests ways to shrink habit duration or reduce barriers. Player commits to specific "easy" implementation.
Friction Reduction Strategies:
•   	2-Minute Rule: Shrink to starter version ("Read 1 page" not "Read 30 min")
•   	Reduce steps: Minimize actions needed to start
•   	Prepare environment: Lay out gym clothes night before
•   	Remove decisions: Pre-decide what, when, where
Feature 13: MBM #3 - Make It Rewarding/Fun ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Explains dopamine and immediate rewards. Suggests personalized reward strategies based on player's Passions list. Player designs their reward system.
Reward Types:
•   	Immediate: Small treat, checkmark, app notification
•   	Variable: Mystery rewards (more engaging than predictable)
•   	Social: Share accomplishment, accountability partner
•   	Intrinsic: Track progress, visualize streak
Feature 14: IF-THEN Obstacle Planning ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Proactively identifies obstacles before they happen. Creates IF-THEN plans: "IF [obstacle], THEN [response]." Uses player's Obstacles list from Momentum Lists.
Example IF-THEN Plans:
•   	IF I'm too tired to workout, THEN I'll do 5 minutes of stretching instead
•   	IF I'm traveling, THEN I'll use hotel gym or do bodyweight exercises
•   	IF I forget my lunch, THEN I'll choose the healthiest option at nearest restaurant
Feature 15: "Momentified" Habit Output ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Combines Golden Habit + 3 MBMs into final "Momentified" habit ready for Phase 2 tracking. Creates summary card showing the complete habit system.
Momentified Habit Card Example:
Golden Habit: Morning 10-minute meditation
Cue (Obvious): After pouring coffee, before checking phone
Easy Version: Start with 2 minutes, build to 10
Reward: Log in app (XP), enjoy coffee mindfully
IF-THEN: If running late, do 2-min breathing instead

4.3 Daily Check-In Features (21-35)
Feature 21: Hybrid Scoring System ❌ [CRITICAL INNOVATION]
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Major innovation that maintains the "Firing on All Cylinders" principle while reducing daily friction. Different scoring for Active Cores vs. Maintaining Cores.
How It Works:
Active Cores (with Golden Habits/Working On habits): Detailed habit-by-habit scoring with specific feedback.
Maintaining Cores (no active habits yet): Quick 1-5 general score for the whole core. "How's your Physical Health today?" Simple, fast.
Why This Matters:
Without this, users would have to score 25+ items daily (5 cores × 5 habits each). With Hybrid Scoring, early users might only score 7 items (2 active habits + 5 quick core scores). As they progress and add more habits, scoring naturally expands.
Feature 22: Interactive Animated Sliders ❌
Status: NOT STARTED
What It Does:
Liquid-filled sliders that respond with haptic feedback. As user slides, liquid fills the container. Score-specific encouragement messages appear. Visual delight makes scoring feel like play, not work.
Score-Specific Messages:
•   	Score 1: "Tough day? Tomorrow's a fresh start. You showed up - that counts! +10 XP"
•   	Score 2: "Not your best, but you're tracking. That's momentum building! +25 XP"
•   	Score 3: "Solid effort! Consistency beats perfection. +50 XP"
•   	Score 4: "Great day! You're building real momentum. +75 XP"
