BRIEF OVERVIEW OF BOTH PHASES OF THE MM SYSTEM
The Moore Momentum System uses a gamified two-phase framework powered by a unified AI engine that transforms personal development from guesswork into a science-backed, personalized, journey that’s both enjoyable and effective.

The two phases bridge the 3 Key Transformative Elements of the MM System by helping you discover:
Awareness (WHO you are):
Pinpoint the pain points and bad habits stifling your growth across all 5 Core Areas.
Vision (WHAT you want):
Create a vivid picture of the fully upgraded version of yourself in all 5 Cores and identify the personalized habits and behavioral science methods to get there.
Action, Accountability, Automaticity (HOW to get it):
Leverage a unified action/accountability system to make bridging the gap between your current and upgraded self fun and inevitable. 
Phase 1 gamifies discovery and setup (your “character build” and mission planning). Phase 2 gamifies execution by turning everything into a daily gameplay loop with a single unified HUD/command center you can access anytime.
Phase 1: AI-Guided Foundation Building (Stages 1 & 2) Discover WHO you are (Awareness), WHAT you want (Vision), and establish the personalized golden habits and science based momentum boosting strategies to get there—guided by AI that learns from your unique profile and thousands of similar users.
Phase 2: AI-Powered Daily Execution Discover HOW to get there (Execution) by converting those personalized habits into consistent action through gamified daily accountability, real-time AI coaching, and predictive interventions that catch struggles before failure.
The 2 phases of the MM System combine to form a one-stop-shop, AI life-coach at your fingertips approach to free you from the clutter of juggling multiple apps and tools addressing various aspects of your life that are hard to track and /boring to stick with. 
This allows you to build the discipline and momentum required to level-up your life so that the growth process becomes something you WANT to do, not have to. 
The AI Advantage:
One Brain, Two Phases: A single AI system powers both discovery and execution, learning bidirectionally—Phase 2 outcomes refine Phase 1 recommendations for all future users.
Gets Smarter Over Time: Every user's success/failure data improves the system for everyone (Data Learning Effects: 70% → 92% accuracy over 36 months).
Hyper-Personalized: AI analyzes your personality, goals, lifestyle, and behavioral patterns to recommend habits with predicted success rates based on thousands of similar users.
NOTE: Predicted success rates are a Post-MVP enhancement (🔶). For MVP, Golden Habits are presented without percentage-based predictions.
Proactive, Not Reactive: AI detects struggling habits before failure through pattern recognition, scores, and Captain's Log analysis—triggering interventions that prevent 30-40% of drop-offs.
Layered Implementation Structure:
Both phases combine three foundational layers:
Gamification: Proven engagement mechanisms that make it fun and rewarding to take action and become ethically addicted to the leveling-up process. 
AI-Powered Personalization: Deep learning that customizes everything to YOU—replacing generic one-size-fits-all advice with tailored recommendations that evolve as you do.
Scientific Validation: Evidence-based concepts from psychology, neuroscience, and behavioral science that confirm and support these principles.
Universal Principles: Timeless truths that serve as the "why" behind change—foundational forces driving personal growth and behavioral momentum.
Through this integrated approach, the MM System ensures sustainable transformation across all 5 Core Areas of Life.

Objective: Identify the pain points slowing the growth/momentum of your 5 Core Areas and determine the optimal personalized "Golden Habits" to overcome them. Then enhance those habits with one or more of the 3 Momentum Boosting Methods until success becomes practically inevitable—all guided by AI that learns from your profile and cross-user patterns.
Approach: Through a combination of science-backed and principle-driven strategies, AI-guided discovery, and personalized habit-building techniques, the system lays the groundwork for lasting behavioral transformation and sustainable momentum.

Use the AI-powered Habits Hierarchy System (HHS) to pinpoint pain points through guided conversation

Goal: Enhance Golden Habit from Phase 1, Stage 1 with friction-reducing, motivation-enhancing strategies proven by science and universal truths—continuing the hyperpersonalizing to overcome top obstacles, leverage opportunities to greatly increase the habit’s success rate.

Make it Obvious/Attractive – Design your environment for success
Make it Easy – Break the habit into small, doable steps
Make it Fun/Rewarding – Create enjoyable feedback loops and celebration rituals
AI-driven IF-THEN planning: System suggests context-specific triggers based on your routine data

Reward matching: AI personalizes "Make it Fun/Rewarding" to your motivation profile (intrinsic vs. extrinsic)

PHASE 1, STAGE 1: DETAILED OVERVIEW AND KEY COMPONENTS 

1. Goal / Overview
Goal:
Create the bridge between a player's current self and their future, upgraded self — by making them deeply aware of what's holding them back and equipping them with a personalized, science-backed solution. Phase 1, Stage 1 accomplishes the first pillar of the MM System's three-part transformation framework: Awareness.

Overview:
Phase 1, Stage 1 is the entry point of the Moore Momentum System. Its purpose is to establish the Awareness pillar using a combination of science-backed strategies, AI-guided discovery, and personalized habit-building techniques to lay the groundwork for lasting behavioral transformation. The approach follows a clear logic:
Identify: Use the AI-powered Habits Hierarchy System to surface pain points through guided, conversational interaction. The AI asks targeted questions that help the player uncover challenges they may not have been consciously aware of.
Personalize: At each step of the Habits Hierarchy, gather personalization data (personality, goals, lifestyle, obstacles) that both refines the habit recommendation and populates the player's Momentum Lists for future use across the entire system.
Validate: Ensure every Golden Habit is grounded in Universal Principles, validated by behavioral science, and cross-referenced against the player's complete profile. The AI also draws on cross-user pattern data to identify what works for similar profiles.
Finalize: Produce a Golden Habit that meets the Triple-Check Criteria (WANTS to do, CAN do, EFFECTIVE) and includes built-in obstacle resilience through "if-then" contingency planning.
Reward & Store: Upon completion, unlock the Command Center with populated Momentum Lists as a tangible reward, reinforcing the gamified experience and providing the player with a visual representation of the self-knowledge they've just built.
Adaptive Pacing: The AI adjusts conversation depth based on engagement patterns. Fast responders get an express mode; detailed responders get deeper exploration. This ensures the experience feels neither rushed nor tedious for any player type.
Starting Simple: This stage emphasizes simplicity, gradual progression, and self-awareness, ensuring players are set up for long-term success without feeling overwhelmed. Players start by identifying just one or two Golden Habits, mastering them one at a time to build confidence and momentum before expanding.

2. Philosophy: Why Phase 1, Stage 1 Exists
The design of Phase 1, Stage 1 is grounded in several core beliefs that differentiate the MM System from conventional self-help:

Without Awareness, There Is No Choice:
Most people know they want to improve but can't pinpoint exactly what's stalling them or which actions will create the biggest impact. Generic advice compounds this problem. Phase 1, Stage 1 exists to cut through information overload and give players crystal-clear awareness of their specific pain points and the precise habits that will address them — validated by behavioral science and universal principles.

Start Small, Build Momentum:
The system deliberately avoids the "jump right in" trap. By focusing on one pain point and one Golden Habit at a time, it reduces overwhelm and applies the science of momentum: small wins build confidence, which builds energy, which fuels the next win. This mirrors Newton's First Law as applied to personal growth — reducing friction to get moving, then leveraging that motion to sustain and accelerate progress.

Personalization Over Prescription:
One-size-fits-all advice is the central failure of the self-help industry. Phase 1, Stage 1 ensures every recommendation is deeply personalized to the player's unique personality, preferences, goals, lifestyle, and obstacles. A Golden Habit that works for one player may be completely wrong for another, even if they share the same pain point. The Habits Hierarchy System ensures each habit passes a Triple-Check: the player WANTS to do it, CAN do it, and it will be EFFECTIVE at addressing their specific challenge.

The Dual-Phase Advantage:
Phase 1, Stage 1 is intentionally separated from the gamified daily execution of Phase 2. This sequenced approach ensures all groundwork — awareness, personalization, friction reduction — is laid before a player is asked to track and build habits daily. By the time they enter Phase 2, they're not guessing at what to do; they have a tailored plan with obstacles already addressed, dramatically improving follow-through.

Ethical Addiction From the Start:
Even though Phase 1, Stage 1 is the onboarding and discovery phase, it incorporates gamified elements from the very first interaction. Quick wins, immediate value, and rewarding progression are built in to hook players on the positive dopamine cycle early, ensuring they're excited to continue their journey rather than dropping off after a one-time assessment.

3. System Integration / How It's Used
AI personalizes every step of the discovery conversation in real time — adjusting pacing and depth based on how the player responds, routing answers to the correct Momentum Lists behind the scenes, and validating each Golden Habit against the player's full profile. 
Gamification runs parallel: each completed HHS section unlocks an achievement title, earns Momentum Points, and advances the visual progress bar — culminating in the Command Center unlock as the Stage 1 reward.
For the full breakdown of how each mechanic works → see the  “Gamification, AI & Science: System Implementation” doc. 
For why these design choices were made → see “Product Design Rationale & Decision Log” doc.

Welcomed and guided through an intuitive, engaging experience that feels more like an adventure than a questionnaire

Design principles for the first-time experience:

Immediate Value: The player walks away from their first session with something concrete — a Golden Habit and a populated Command Center — not just a promise of future value
Frictionless Flow: The experience should feel effortless; the AI carries the conversational weight and the player simply responds naturally

An abbreviated version of each appears during the daily review/scoring ritual — both Routine and Non-Routine versions — which the player then scores. They are also accessible in all other areas where the applicable Golden Habit and its MBMs are found:

AI Flagging / Coaching Engine:
When a habit is struggling — defined as a score of 3 or below for 3 consecutive days and/or Captain's Log comments triggering an AI intervention — the system notifies the player and asks whether they'd like to adjust their Golden Habit and/or its attached MBMs. Three options are offered:

🚀 Quick Suggestion — AI suggests a fast tweak based on what it already knows about the player (previous conversations and Momentum Lists). No additional questions asked.
🔬 Go Deeper — Revisit and potentially rebuild the Golden Habit by returning to the HHS (Phase 1, Stage 1) and/or the MBMs by returning to the MBS (Phase 1, Stage 2). The system pre-loads all relevant context so the player doesn't repeat what's already been captured.
✏️ Manual Edit — Player manually adjusts the Golden Habit directly, with the option to overwrite the existing tag or save as a revised version with a date stamp.

Access:
Direct links within the Daily Review / Scoring Routine for quick in-context access when a habit needs adjustment
Permanent access via the Hamburger Menu on the default rocket dashboard (introduced in Phase 2)

Storage & Location
Each Golden Habit is tagged and stored in the Detailed Golden Habits section of the platform for easy reference and future refinement — as well as for the MM AI System to use for context in all interactions with players moving forward. Momentum List data is stored in Firebase and API-integrated across all phases so information entered in any section is immediately reflected system-wide.

4. Key Components Of Phase 1, Stage 1
Phase 1, Stage 1 permanently unlocks three foundational components of the MM System. Each is introduced here and carries forward through all subsequent phases and stages.
4A: Habits Hierarchy System (HHS)
Phase 1, Stage 1 — Primary Component

Goal / Overview:

Goal: Identify the key pain points, challenges, and bad habits stalling momentum and happiness in each of the 5 Core Areas of Life, then match each with a personalized Golden Habit for relief.

Overview: Players progress through the 5-Section Habits Hierarchy (6 for first-time players) refining and personalizing their habits at each stage. By the time they reach their Golden Habit, it is fully tailored for lasting change. The HHS is the backbone of Phase 1, Stage 1 — it's the mechanism by which the system moves from generic to personal, from awareness to action-ready.

10k Approach: Phase 1, Stage 1 uses a combination of science-backed strategies, AI-guided discovery, and personalized habit-building techniques to lay the groundwork for lasting behavioral transformation. The approach follows a clear logic:
Identify: Use the AI-powered Habits Hierarchy System to surface pain points through guided, conversational interaction. The AI asks targeted questions that help the player uncover challenges they may not have been consciously aware of.
Personalize: At each step of the Habits Hierarchy, gather personalization data (personality, goals, lifestyle, obstacles) that both refines the habit recommendation and populates the player's Momentum Lists for future use across the entire system.
Validate: Ensure every Golden Habit is grounded in Universal Principles, validated by behavioral science, and cross-referenced against the player's complete profile. The AI also draws on cross-user pattern data to identify what works for similar profiles.
Finalize: Produce a Golden Habit that meets the Triple-Check Criteria (WANTS to do, CAN do, EFFECTIVE) and includes built-in obstacle resilience through "if-then" contingency planning.
Reward & Store: Upon completion, unlock the Command Center with populated Momentum Lists as a tangible reward, reinforcing the gamified experience and providing the player with a visual representation of the self-knowledge they've just built.
Adaptive Pacing: The AI adjusts conversation depth based on engagement patterns. Fast responders get an express mode; detailed responders get deeper exploration. This ensures the experience feels neither rushed nor tedious for any player type.

System Integration / How It's Used:

Initial Use:
After initial introducting/onboarding, the player is guided through the 6-Section HHS with AI-guided questions to identify a top pain point and produce a personalized Golden Habit.
Their responses are tagged and stored in applicable Momentum Lists, which are revealed at the end of Section 6 as a reward permanently unlocked.

Ongoing Use:
The HHS/Golden Habits generator is always available for players to tackle additional pain points across all 5 Core Areas as they become aware of them.
In Phase 1, Stage 2, MBMs are added to each Golden Habit for further friction reduction.
The Golden Habits given are a first draft — as the player tries them out in Phase 2, they can return to the HHS at any time to revamp them.
As the player progresses, both in the game and in real life, they refine and expand their Golden Habits — powering ongoing personalization, optimization, and growth.

Storage & Location:
Each Golden Habit is tagged and stored in the Golden Habits section of the platform (accessed in several areas in the dashboard which are introduced in Phase 2)  for easy reference and future refinement, as well as for the MM AI System to use as context in all interactions with players going forward.

Step/Section 1: Pain Point / Challenge / Bad Habit Identification

Overview:
Initiates the player's journey by surfacing a specific, emotionally resonant pain point or bad habit that's draining momentum. The challenge must be habit-addressable and serve as the foundation for transformation throughout the rest of the Habits Hierarchy. The goal isn't just identification — it's to anchor the journey in something real, current, and changeable.

Purpose:
Surface and clarify one core pain point or momentum-draining habit that is deeply felt by the player right now
Ensure the pain point can be addressed through consistent behavior change
Clearly map the pain point to one of the 5 Core Areas of Life
Give the MM System a powerful starting point to personalize every step forward — ensuring relevance, emotional buy-in, and measurable transformation

Step/Section 4: Keystone Habit Personalization

Overview:
The first major personalization step in the Habits Hierarchy, transforming the broad Core Dimension (from Section 3) into a tailored Keystone Habit Category. Pulls insights from the player's motivations, lifestyle, and ideal identity — while also determining habit type (Routine vs. Non-Routine) and cue/trigger. Bridges emotional insight into action by aligning the habit with what the player WANTS to do, CAN do, and what will be EFFECTIVE — before locking in specifics in Section 5.

Purpose:
Define the type of habit (Routine or Non-Routine), its cue/trigger, and a highly personalized Keystone Habit Category that fits the player's identity, motivation, and lifestyle
Ask 1 personalized question per Golden Habit Criterion (WANT, CAN, EFFECTIVE), using logic from prior answers — up to one follow-up per question if needed
Confirm habit classification (Routine vs. Non-Routine) and capture an early cue or anchor moment to reduce friction
Present a motivating, realistic Keystone Habit Category with an explanation of why it works
Tag all relevant Momentum Lists to power downstream optimization

Step/Section 5: Golden Habit Finalization

Overview:
This final section of Phase 1, Stage 1 transforms the Keystone Habit Category (from Section 4) into a Golden Habit — a highly specific, low-friction behavior the player can implement immediately with confidence and clarity. 
Strengthens all 3 Golden Habit Criteria: WANT to do (intrinsically motivating), CAN do (lifestyle fit, clear cue, built-in backup plan), Will be EFFECTIVE (supports identity growth). 
Locks in the cue/trigger with proper Routine vs. Non-Routine classification, an IF/THEN Obstacle Plan backed by one of the 3 MBMs, a motivational "Why This Works" breakdown, and an ultra-simple Starting Version for immediate implementation.

Purpose:
Finalize a habit that actually sticks — one that's simple, powerful, and crafted directly from the player's own insights
Bridge the gap between planning and doing by sharpening the WHAT, WHEN, WHERE, and TRIGGER
Anticipate resistance with a personalized MBM-backed fallback
Anchor the habit in identity, lifestyle, and long-term motivation
The output must feel like the player's own idea — natural, achievable, and exciting to follow through on

Steps Involved:

🔵 First-Time Players:
The "Why This Works" breakdown (WANT / CAN / EFFECTIVE) is explained as a concept here — the player learns what the three criteria mean before seeing their habit validated against them.
Starting Version / atomic habit concept is introduced for the first time.

Reuse prior data aggressively; the refinement questions are only asked where new information is genuinely required. Obstacle planning is faster since the player has used IF-THEN before. The Starting Version is offered but not introduced as a new concept.

Determine what already fulfills the outputs needed — only ask questions where new info is truly required

Step 2: Ask 2–4 Refinement Questions — Only If Needed

Q1 — WANT to Do: Confirm the habit is aligned with something the player is energized by. Reuse Section 4 answer if specific and clearly boosts motivation; only ask a follow-up if vague or abstract.
Tag → Passions List / Strengths List

Q2 — CAN Do: Confirm the Golden Habit has a crystal-clear cue/trigger — the actual moment of habit initiation, distinct from any Anchor Reminder. Reuse Section 4 cue if specific, observable, and properly classified. Refine only if WHEN or WHERE is vague or missing.

Q3 — Will Be EFFECTIVE: Confirm the habit aligns with the player's aspirational identity. Typically does NOT need follow-up unless Section 4 answer was particularly vague.
Tag → Back to the Future List (final)

Step 3: Obstacle Plan with Applicable MBM
Ask the player to identify one top likely obstacle (internal or external) that could derail the habit
Generate 3 MBM-backed fallback strategies, each with a clear IF/THEN structure:
1️⃣ Make It Obvious/Invisible
2️⃣ Make It Easy/Difficult
3️⃣ Make It Rewarding/Punishing
Include at least one "Set It and Forget It" option if applicable. Reuse any existing Anchor Reminder from Section 4 if the obstacle is "forgetting in the moment."
Player selects, modifies, or writes their own
⚠️ Do NOT recommend habit trackers or any MM System features coming in later stages/phases
Tag → Obstacles List (with Internal/External subtag), If-Then Strategy List (with MBM category)

Step 4: Format the Final Golden Habit Output
Assemble the complete Golden Habit using all validated responses. Must include:
📋 HABIT TYPE — Routine or Non-Routine
📍 WHERE — environment/context
📅 WHEN — cue/trigger moment
🚀 WHAT — the exact small, specific action
⚓️💡 ANCHOR REMINDER — only if applicable (Non-Routine habits)
⚠️ OBSTACLE PLAN — IF obstacle → THEN MBM-backed fallback
🪄✨ WHY THIS WORKS — WANT / CAN / EFFECTIVE breakdown
Use confident first-person phrasing ("I will" not "I'll try to"). Cue language should be clear, sensory, and context-specific.

Step 5: Confirm with Player
Option to lock in the habit or make adjustments

Goal / Overview:

Goal: Ensure every Golden Habit is correctly classified as either a Routine Habit or a Non-Routine Habit so the system can apply the right formation strategy, organizational structure, tracking method, and MBM recommendations from day one.

Overview: The Routine vs. Non-Routine Habit Classification is the organizational backbone of the MM System — the structure that determines where habits live in a player's actual day and how they're triggered. It's introduced in Section 4, where the AI helps determine whether the player's Golden Habit is externally triggered (Routine) or internally triggered (Non-Routine), then validates with the player before locking it in.

This classification matters because the two habit types require fundamentally different approaches to formation, and applying the wrong strategy is one of the most common reasons habits fail. A habit that looks routine on the surface (e.g., "I stress eat at my desk every afternoon") may actually be non-routine because the real trigger is emotional (stress), not environmental (the desk). Getting the classification right ensures the player receives correctly matched MBMs, organizational placement, and scoring approach.
Core Philosophy — From Default Mode to Design Mode:
Most people live by default, not by design. Their routine and non-routine habits are shaped by past circumstances, external pressures, and the path of least resistance—quietly reinforcing patterns they never consciously chose. As a result, they drift through life on autopilot rather than intentionally architecting who they’re becoming.
The challenge is that only a small fraction of these habits are visible. The rest operate beneath awareness, subtly shaping identity and outcomes across all 5 Core Areas of Life. Since habits are running either way, the real question becomes: Are they moving the player toward the life they want, or keeping them stuck where they are?
The MM System exists to solve this autopilot problem. It helps players surface what’s invisible, strengthen what’s working, and systematically replace what isn’t—shifting them from passive fixed-victim passenger to proactive growth-owner architect. That’s why Awareness, the first of the three key pillars, must come first. Without awareness, Vision cannot be clarified and Action/Accountability cannot be effectively activated.
Within that awareness is crucial to understand that difference between Routine and Non-Routine habits. 
The Habit Loop Framework:
All habits— both routine and non-routine — follow the same Habit Loop: Cue/Trigger → Craving → Response → Reward. The difference is how each stage works depending on the type:
The Two Foundational Habit Types:

Routine Habits (✅⏰) — Externally Triggered:
Definition: Tied to a specific time and place (e.g., "Protein shake after making coffee in the kitchen at 7:15 AM")
Why They're Easier to Form: Environmental cues trigger them automatically — the neural pathways already exist for current habits, so the new behavior piggybacks on what's already wired. Replacing existing habits is easier than starting from scratch. 
Organization: By time block — Morning, Afternoon, Evening — with habits grouped into environmental clusters (Kitchen, Workstation, Bedroom, etc.) within each block
Primary Formation Method: Habit Stacking — "After I [current habit], I will [new habit]"
How the Habit Loop Applies:
Cue: A consistent environmental trigger (time, place, preceding action) that fires the sequence automatically
Craving: Anticipation built through repetition — the brain begins expecting the new behavior as part of the existing pattern
Response: The stacked habit executes as part of an automated sequence
Reward: Scheduled reinforcement (points, streaks, progress tracking) delivered at completion
Color-Coding: 🔴 Red (bad habit), 🟠 Orange (Golden Habit in progress), 🟢 Green (formed - stays for the visual “sea of green” aesthetic, and also goes into trophy room ), ⚫ Black (neutral/opportunity), 🔵 Blue (MBM), 🌟 Yellow (notes)

Non-Routine Habits (❌⏰) — Internally Triggered:
Definition: Can happen anytime, anywhere (e.g., "Practice active listening in conversations," "Manage stress spiraling")
Why They're Harder to Form: They run on emotional autopilot — lacking consistent time/place triggers and rarely getting examined
Organization: By Core Area in priority order (highest-damage failure habits at the top) — not by time of day. Drag-and-drop to reprioritize.
Primary Formation Method: IF-THEN Framework — IF [internal trigger/emotion], THEN [growth-owner response]
How the Habit Loop Applies:
Cue: An internal state — thought, emotion, or physical sensation — that initiates the behavioral pattern
Craving: The emotional pull toward the familiar (often unhealthy) response
Response: A flexible, pre-planned growth-owner behavior replacing the default reaction
Reward: Immediate emotional validation — the player recognizes the win of responding by design rather than by default
Color-Coding: Streamlined — 🔴 Red (bad), 🟠 Orange (in progress), 🔵 Blue (MBM), ✅ Formed (goes into trophy room). No ⚫ Black — every non-routine habit is either helping or hurting.

The Classification Key Test:
If the underlying trigger is emotional (stress, anxiety, boredom, criticism) → Non-Routine, even if it sometimes occurs in a consistent location
"I stress eat at my desk every afternoon" → Looks routine (desk, afternoon), but trigger is stress → Non-Routine
"I skip making my bed every morning" → Trigger is time/place-based → Routine
"I shut down during difficult conversations" → Trigger is emotional (defensiveness, fear) → Non-Routine

Misclassification Safety Net:
If a player is repeatedly scoring low on a habit, the system checks whether it's correctly classified as part of the diagnostic process. Players can reclassify at any time, which automatically updates the organizational structure, MBM strategies, and scoring approach. When reclassified:

The Golden Habit carries over
MBMs are flagged for review and the system walks the player through adapting strategies to the new classification
Scoring history is preserved but marked with a "reclassified on [date]" note so trends aren't misleading

This isn't about forcing behavior; it's about redesigning the system so the desired behavior emerges naturally.

Most habit failure stems from a handful of common friction patterns: time pressure, environmental misalignment, competing priorities, social barriers, and emotional resistance. Section 3.2: MBM 2 — Make It Easy

Habit Loop Stage Addressed: Response (taking action)
Focus: Reduce the friction of executing the habit by breaking it into its smallest possible form, anchoring it to existing behaviors, and reframing the time/effort investment accurately — so that the path of least resistance leads to the habit, not away from it.

The 3 Strategies:
Habit Stacking: Anchor the new habit onto an existing automatic behavior. Formula: "After I [current habit], I will [new habit]." Default strategy for Routine Habits.
Proactive Friction Reduction: Remove all upstream obstacles before they arise, while adding friction to competing behaviors. Includes One-Time Setup Actions and MVA backups.
MVA (Minimal Viable Action): Define the smallest possible action that still activates the habit loop — a floor the player can always hit. Includes tiered backup system (e.g., 5 reps → 3 reps → 10 arm circles).

Celebrate Wins: Build in an immediate, intentional micro-celebration at the moment of completion — trains the brain to associate the habit with positive emotion rather than obligation

2: Philosophy: Why Phase 2 Exists
Design Philosophy
Every design decision in Phase 2 answers one question: "Will the player want to come back tomorrow?"
The daily review must feel like a game, not a chore. The system always rewards effort, never punishes failure. Consistency is celebrated, struggles are met with empathy, and even showing up on a difficult day is treated as progress.
The design of Phase 2 is grounded in several core beliefs that differentiate the MM System from every conventional habit tracker and self-improvement app on the market.

The Daily Review/Scoring Ritual as Transformation Engine, Not Just a Tracker
Most habit apps are glorified checklists — they record what you did but do nothing to change who you are. Phase 2's daily ritual is engineered to serve five simultaneous functions:
Single-Pass Scoring: Players review each habit once during scoring — the act of reflecting on yesterday naturally primes thinking for today, eliminating the need for a separate planning step. Full habit reference remains available on-demand via the ALL HABITS QUICK VIEW (∞ icon on the rocket dashboard) without interrupting the morning ritual.
Backward Reflection: Review yesterday's performance across all 5 Cores to build self-awareness and pattern recognition — connecting actions to consequences in real time.
Forward Priming: The act of scoring yesterday naturally prepares the player's mind for today without forced repetition. No separate 'plan today' step required.
Bridge Between Current Self And Future Self (Reverse Engineering Habits To Ensure Growth): Daily connection between current habits and the Back to the Future vision of the player’s future leveled-up vision of themselves strengthens the new identity being built — 'this is who I'm becoming' rather than 'this is what I have to do.'
Friction-Minimized Habit Display: Habits appear as abbreviated names during scoring—not full context—keeping the ritual achievable in 5–10 minutes. Players already know their habits; full BTTF connections, MBMs, obstacle plans, and setup details are available on-demand via a [Detailed View] button, preventing information overload without sacrificing depth.
Core-Level Reflection Architecture and Pattern Detection: The Captain's Log captures a single wins & lessons entry per Core—not per habit—mirroring how players naturally think ("how did my mindset go?") rather than forcing micro-reflections on every individual habit. This feeds the AI more actionable pattern data while keeping the ritual fast and cognitively sustainable.
Calibrated Scoring Scale: A 1–5 scale balances nuance with clarity—capturing meaningful variation in performance quality (not just completion) without triggering decision paralysis. Each number carries a distinct, memorable meaning, and the 7-day rolling average across all 5 Cores powers the Balance Meter. Binary (yes/no) was rejected for flattening effort; 1–10 was rejected for creating meaningless distinctions.
Momentum Building: Gamification elements (streaks, points, rewards) create ethical addiction that fuels consistent execution and makes showing up tomorrow feel worth it.
What Players Are Building Through This Ritual:
The daily ritual is not just about tracking habits—it's about systematic transformation:
🟢 A sea of green automated habits
 Routine habits that turn green represent the foundation of an automatic, friction-free daily schedule.
🏆 A Trophy Room full of formed habits
 Non-routine habits (and all habits) that reach "formed" status become permanent identity markers, visually celebrated.
🚀 A leveled-up rocket ship
 Wings, armor, and thrusters earned through consistency create both visual progress and functional gameplay benefits.
🎯 Balanced growth across all 5 Cores
 The Balance Meter prevents the common pitfall of excelling in one area while neglecting others.
💪 An unshakeable growth-owner identity
 Through repeated daily practice of scoring and reflection, players rewire their core identity from "fixed victim" to "growth owner."
🌌 A completely transformed life
 The compound effect of daily wins across all 5 Cores creates holistic life transformation—"firing on all cylinders."
The result: A 5–10 minute ritual versus 10–15 minutes in competing systems, with less cognitive load, more natural flow, and player choice about when to reference habits. This is one of the core innovations that differentiates Phase 2 from every other habit tracking system.
____
Identity Over Activity: Who You're Becoming vs. What You're Doing
The Back to the Future (BTTF) vision shown at the top of each Core during scoring isn't decoration — it's behavioral science applied to identity transformation. 
By anchoring each habit to a vivid future self (rather than framing it as a task to complete), the system shifts the player's internal narrative from 'I'm doing this habit' to 'I'm becoming this person.' 
Grounded in identity-based habit theory (James Clear) and growth mindset research (Carol Dweck), Phase 2 treats every check-in, every Captain's Log reflection, and every formed habit as accumulating evidence of the new identity being built. 
Over time, this shifts players from 'I'm someone trying to exercise' to 'I am someone who prioritizes physical health' — the difference between effortful compliance and automatic behavior that feels natural.
____
Ethical Gamification: Dopamine Tied to Real Progress
The same dopamine-driven mechanisms that fuel social media and unhealthy video game addiction are fully present in Phase 2 — but redirected toward genuine behavioral transformation. Every reward is verified against real behavioral data: you cannot earn a Mystery Box for opening the app, inflate a score without Captain's Log corroboration, or buy your way to the next planet with Space Credits.
This creates what the MM System calls ethical addiction — the player becomes hooked on the growth process itself, not a hollow simulation of it.
____
Flexible Rigor: Accountability Without Shame
Phase 2 is designed around a core tension: accountability must have real teeth, but punishment and shame reliably cause abandonment. The solution is a system that escalates consequences compassionately, preserves core progress through any setback, and always provides a clear, non-shaming recovery pathway.
One missed check-in gets a warning and encouragement — no consequences. A second consecutive miss triggers a real consequence (rocket checkpoint regression) while keeping Trophy Room, credits, and habit formation status fully intact. The framing is always "relaunching," never "failing." Multiple streak protection layers (Weekend Protection, Streak Savers, Armor upgrades, Vacation Mode) ensure that genuine life disruptions don't undo months of effort.
____
Compound Growth Across All 5 Cores
Phase 2 is the enforcement mechanism for the MM System's foundational promise: holistic, balanced growth across all 5 Core Areas of Life. The scoring structure, the Balance Meter, the Core Balance Alert system, and the Balance Bonuses built into the Momentum Points economy all exist to ensure that no single Core is neglected at the expense of others.
The Ripple Effect — one of the MM System's core principles — only operates when all 5 Cores are receiving attention. Phase 2's architecture makes 'firing on all cylinders' not just a goal but a daily, measurable, rewarded outcome.

4C: Return Access to Phase 1
Goal / Overview:
Goal: Ensure Phase 1 never becomes a closed door — the MM System's long-term power comes from two equally important feedback loops:
The daily scoring ritual surfacing when an existing habit needs to be rebuilt or re-engineered.
The player's continuously deepening self-awareness driving them to proactively identify new pain points and build new Golden Habits for them.
Overview: Both Stage 1 (Habits Hierarchy System) and Stage 2 (Momentum Boosting System) remain permanently accessible from Phase 2 at any time. As the player progresses through Phase 2, two complementary forces keep pulling them back into Phase 1 — one reactive, one proactive — and together they ensure the system never goes stale and always reflects who the player is right now.
________
The Ongoing Awareness Journey: The Proactive Driver
One of the three foundational pillars of the MM System is Awareness — and awareness is not a one-time event. It deepens continuously. A player who starts the system with one pain point in their Physical Health Core will, over weeks and months of daily reflection through the Captain's Log and scoring ritual, begin to clearly see pain points in their Career Core, their Relationships Core, their Mindset. What was invisible at the start becomes obvious once momentum is built and the noise clears.
This is by design. Phase 2's daily ritual — the scoring, the Captain's Log, the Balance Meter, the BTTF vision — is as much a self-awareness engine as it is a habit tracker. Every check-in teaches the player more about who they are, where they're stuck, and what their next most impactful move is.
When a player gains this new clarity, Phase 1, Stage 1 is where they go to act on it:
They identify a new pain point in a Core they haven't addressed yet.
They run it through the Habits Hierarchy System to ensure the habit they build is the optimal, personalized Golden Habit for that specific challenge — not a guess, not a generic recommendation.
They then go through Phase 1, Stage 2 to attach the right MBMs, engineering the habit for maximum friction reduction before it ever hits the daily ritual.
The completed Golden Habit + MBMs then populate directly into the daily scoring ritual for that Core — adding a new layer to their execution system.
This cycle — awareness deepens → new pain point identified → Phase 1 builds the optimal habit → Phase 2 executes it daily — is the MM System's core growth loop. It repeats indefinitely, with each pass building a richer, more holistic habit portfolio across all 5 Cores until the player is genuinely firing on all cylinders.
The more the player progresses, the more valuable each Phase 1 return becomes: their Momentum Lists are richer, the AI has more data, and the resulting Golden Habits are increasingly precise and effective.
________
The Habit Flagging System: The Reactive Driver
The second reason players return to Phase 1 is the daily scoring ritual's built-in intervention mechanism — a hybrid AI + player flagging system that surfaces when an existing habit isn't working.
How Flags Are Triggered:
AI Auto-Flag: AI detects the player struggling (scored 3 or lower for 3+ consecutive check-ins, and/or Captain's Log comments show recurring negative themes). The AI asks targeted questions to narrow in on which specific Golden Habit(s) and/or MBM(s) are causing friction.
Player Manual Flag: Player clicks [⚠️ Flag This Habit] next to any Golden Habit or MBM at any time — even when scores are above 3. Players know context that AI can't always detect: human intuition identifies root causes that data alone might miss.
Both flagging types display a ⚠️ indicator in all views (Routines List, Non-Routines List, Detailed Habit View, All Habits list) and are prioritized in AI pattern detection.

[🚀 Quick Suggestion]: AI draws from Momentum Lists data and offers 1–2 tailored tweaks. Fast, low-friction. No Phase 1 re-entry required. Changes tagged as a 🧪 Experiment.
[🔬 Go Deeper]: Player selects this when a quick tweak isn't enough — either the habit needs rebuilding (Phase 1, Stage 1) or the MBMs need a full overhaul (Phase 1, Stage 2). See below.
[✏️ Manual Edit]: Player edits the habit or MBMs directly. Saved as a 🧪 Experiment. Player can switch to Go Deeper at any time if the manual edit doesn't resolve the issue.
In all three paths: Changes reflect everywhere applicable — daily scoring ritual, Routines/Non-Routines Lists, All Habits list, Detailed Habit View, and relevant Momentum Lists.

Space Credits are the reward currency — earned through check-ins, quest completions, habit formation milestones, streak rewards, and Mystery Box drops — and are spent on ship upgrades and cosmetic enhancements.
Separating the two ensures no player can buy their way through the journey. Progression must be earned through behavior; credits reward engagement. Level multipliers (1.25x for Navigators, 1.5x for Commanders) reward long-term dedication.
